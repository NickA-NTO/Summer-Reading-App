# If You Give a Mouse a Cookie

**Author:** Laura Joffe Numeroff
**Illustrator:** Felicia Bond
**First published:** 1985
**Grade band:** Grade K / Ages 4–8
**Format:** Illustrated picture book (first in the *If You Give…* series)

---

## Premise

A funny chain-reaction picture book about a small boy and a hungry **mouse**. The book begins, **"If you give a mouse a cookie, he's going to ask for a glass of milk."** Each thing the mouse asks for leads naturally to the next: a straw, a napkin, a mirror, nail scissors, a broom, a blanket, a story, paper and crayons, sticky tape — and finally a glass of milk, which will, of course, mean he is going to ask for another **cookie**. The story is a circle that ends exactly where it began.

## Setting

The boy's house — kitchen, sofa/living room, bathroom mirror, fridge — all on one quiet afternoon. The pictures show the mouse small enough to climb on shelves and chairs.

## Characters

- **The little boy** — never named. Quietly does whatever the mouse asks for.
- **The mouse** — small, friendly, very demanding. Wears blue dungarees in the illustrations.

## The chain of events, in order

1. **A cookie.** The boy gives the mouse a **cookie**.
2. **A glass of milk.** Once the mouse has the cookie, he will of course want **a glass of milk** to go with it.
3. **A straw.** With the milk comes the need for a **straw**.
4. **A napkin.** Drinking with the straw means he'll need a **napkin** to wipe his mouth.
5. **A mirror.** He'll want to check that he doesn't have a **milk moustache** by looking in a **mirror**.
6. **Nail scissors.** Looking in the mirror, he'll notice his hair needs a trim — so he'll ask for **nail scissors**.
7. **A broom.** Trimming his hair will leave a mess on the floor, so he'll want a **broom** to sweep it all up.
8. **A mop.** After sweeping, he'll probably want to **mop the floor** too.
9. **A nap.** All this work will make him tired, so he'll ask if he can have a **little nap** on the sofa.
10. **A pillow and a blanket.** For the nap he'll need a **pillow** and a **blanket** and a small bed to sleep in.
11. **A story.** Before he goes to sleep, he'll ask the boy to read him a **story**.
12. **A picture in his head.** The story will make him want to draw one of his own.
13. **Paper and crayons.** So he'll ask for **paper** and **crayons** and draw a picture.
14. **A pen.** When he finishes the picture, he'll want a **pen** to sign his name.
15. **Tape on the refrigerator.** He'll ask the boy to **stick the picture to the refrigerator** with **tape** so they can both look at it.
16. **Thirsty again.** Looking at the picture on the fridge will make him thirsty.
17. **A glass of milk.** So he'll ask for a **glass of milk**.
18. **And of course…** If you give a mouse a glass of milk, he's going to want a **cookie** to go with it. **The story starts again.**

## Key objects and props

- **The cookie** — where it all begins.
- **The glass of milk** — the second link in the chain (and the start of the loop at the end).
- **The straw, napkin, mirror, nail scissors, broom, mop, blanket, pillow, story, paper, crayons, pen, tape, refrigerator** — the rest of the chain.
- **The fridge** — where the mouse's drawing ends up.

## Themes and ideas

- **One thing leads to another.** A simple gift starts a long, funny chain reaction.
- **Cause and effect.** The book is often used to teach how one action causes the next.
- **Circular stories.** The book ends where it started, with a mouse asking for a cookie.
- **Sharing and patience.** The boy never gets frustrated. He just keeps helping the mouse.
- **A friendly mouse.** The mouse is demanding but never rude — he's polite, hopeful, and clearly enjoying himself.

## Tone and style

A short, funny picture book in second person ("**If you give**… he's going to ask…"). Every sentence flows into the next, just as one cookie flows into a glass of milk, a straw, and so on. The illustrations show a friendly mouse hopping around a child's house in dungarees. The book reads quickly and is fun to predict.

## Quick fact bank (useful for question writing)

- The book's opening line: **"If you give a mouse a cookie, he's going to ask for a glass of milk."**
- What the mouse asks for after the milk: a **straw**.
- What he asks for after the straw: a **napkin**.
- Why he wants a mirror: to check for a **milk moustache**.
- What he asks for to trim his hair: **nail scissors**.
- What he wants to sweep up his hair clippings: a **broom**.
- What he wants after sweeping: a **mop** to mop the floor.
- What he wants when he is tired: a **nap**, with a **pillow** and a **blanket**.
- What he asks for before he falls asleep: a **story**.
- What he wants to make of his own: a **picture** (so he asks for **paper and crayons**).
- What he wants to do with his picture: **stick it on the refrigerator** with **tape**.
- Why looking at the refrigerator makes him thirsty: it reminds him of milk.
- What he asks for next: a **glass of milk**.
- And of course, with the milk he'll want: a **cookie** — bringing the whole story back to where it started.
- The book is the **first** in the **If You Give…** series.
