# Goldilocks and the Three Bears

**Author and Illustrator:** James Marshall (retelling of the classic fairy tale)
**First published:** 1988
**Grade band:** Grade K / Ages 4–7
**Format:** Illustrated picture book (Caldecott Honor)

---

## Premise

A funny, modern retelling of the classic fairy tale. A naughty little girl named **Goldilocks** ignores her mother's warning, takes a shortcut through the woods, and stumbles into the empty house of the **Three Bears**. While they are out for a bicycle ride, she tries their three bowls of **porridge**, their three **chairs**, and their three **beds**, breaking and ruining several things along the way. The bears come home to find her fast asleep in **Baby Bear's bed**. When they wake her up, she screams and runs away. The book ends with the bears tidying up and Goldilocks's mother promising to keep her away from those woods.

## Setting

A countryside between Goldilocks's house, the **woods**, and the **Three Bears' cottage** somewhere in the middle of the woods. Inside the bears' cottage there is a kitchen with the three porridge bowls, a sitting room with the three chairs, and a bedroom upstairs with the three beds. Time: one morning while the bears are out cycling.

## Characters

- **Goldilocks** — a little girl with golden curls. Disobedient, nosy, and not at all sorry for herself.
- **Goldilocks's mother** — sends Goldilocks on an errand and tells her to stay on the main path.
- **Papa Bear** — the biggest of the three bears. Has the largest bowl, the largest chair, and the largest bed. Wears **bunny slippers** in this version, which is one of Marshall's added jokes.
- **Mama Bear** — the middle-sized bear. Has the middle-sized bowl, chair, and bed.
- **Baby Bear** — the smallest of the bears. His bowl, his chair, and his bed are the smallest.

## Plot in order

1. **The warning.** Goldilocks's mother sends her on an errand — to **buy muffins** at the next village. She warns her not to take the **shortcut through the woods** because there are **bears** in there.
2. **The shortcut.** Goldilocks ignores the warning and takes the shortcut anyway.
3. **The bears' breakfast.** Meanwhile, **Papa Bear, Mama Bear, and Baby Bear** are about to have **porridge** for breakfast in their cottage, but the porridge is **too hot**. They decide to go for a **bicycle ride** on their three-seat bicycle and come back when the porridge has cooled.
4. **An empty cottage.** Goldilocks wanders through the woods and finds the bears' cottage. There is **no one home**. She lets herself in.
5. **The three porridges.** On the kitchen table she finds **three bowls of porridge**. She tries Papa Bear's — **too hot**. She tries Mama Bear's — **too cold**. She tries Baby Bear's — **just right** — and **eats it all up**.
6. **The three chairs.** In the sitting room she finds **three chairs**. Papa Bear's is **too hard**. Mama Bear's is **too soft**. Baby Bear's is **just right** — but she sits down so hard on it that **it breaks to pieces**.
7. **The three beds.** Upstairs she finds **three beds**. Papa Bear's is **too hard**. Mama Bear's is **too soft**. Baby Bear's is **just right**. She climbs in and falls **fast asleep**.
8. **The bears come home.** The three bears arrive back from their bicycle ride. They go into the kitchen and notice their porridge has been disturbed. **"Somebody has been eating my porridge!"** says Papa Bear. **"Somebody has been eating my porridge!"** says Mama Bear. **"Somebody has been eating my porridge — and they've eaten it all up!"** says Baby Bear.
9. **The broken chair.** They go to the sitting room. Each chair has been moved. Baby Bear's is in pieces. **"Somebody has been sitting in my chair — and they've broken it to bits!"** cries Baby Bear.
10. **Up the stairs.** The bears head upstairs. Papa Bear says somebody's been in his bed. Mama Bear says somebody's been in her bed. Baby Bear says somebody's been in **his** bed — **"and she's still in it!"**
11. **Goldilocks wakes up.** Goldilocks opens her eyes, sees three large bears looming over her, **screams**, and jumps out of the window (or runs straight out the door) as fast as she can.
12. **Home again.** Goldilocks runs all the way home through the woods. Her mother is relieved to see her and tells her she will **never let her go that way again**.
13. **The bears, meanwhile,** tidy up the cottage and have a quiet day. The book closes with the wry suggestion that little girls should listen to their mothers.

## Key objects and props

- **The bears' three-seat bicycle** — Marshall's joke addition.
- **Three bowls of porridge** — too hot, too cold, just right.
- **Three chairs** — too hard, too soft, just right (and broken).
- **Three beds** — too hard, too soft, just right.
- **Papa Bear's bunny slippers** — a Marshall touch.
- **The window** Goldilocks leaps from at the end.

## Themes and ideas

- **Listen to your mother.** Goldilocks ignores a clear warning and ends up in real trouble.
- **Don't help yourself to other people's things.** The whole story is one long lesson on this point.
- **"Just right."** The "too hot / too cold / just right" pattern (and chairs, and beds) is the heart of the book's structure.
- **Repetition.** The same three-step pattern repeats — kitchen, sitting room, bedroom — making it easy for children to predict and join in.
- **Comic touches.** This Marshall version makes the bears affectionately silly: a three-seat bike, bunny slippers, scolding wives, and a porridge breakfast.

## Tone and style

A short, funny picture book in James Marshall's loose cartoon style. The bears wear human clothes and look gently ridiculous. Dialogue is bright and the patterns ("too hot / too cold / just right") repeat exactly each time, so a child can join in on every page.

## Quick fact bank (useful for question writing)

- The little girl's name: **Goldilocks**.
- The errand Goldilocks is supposed to be running: buying **muffins** at the next village.
- What her mother tells her **not** to do: take a **shortcut through the woods** (because of the bears).
- The three members of the bear family: **Papa Bear, Mama Bear, and Baby Bear**.
- Why the bears leave the cottage: their **porridge is too hot** to eat.
- What the bears go off to do while it cools: ride their **three-seat bicycle** through the woods.
- A funny detail about Papa Bear in this version: he wears **bunny slippers**.
- The first thing Goldilocks finds in the kitchen: **three bowls of porridge**.
- Whose porridge Goldilocks ate all up: **Baby Bear's**.
- The "rule of three" with the porridge: Papa Bear's is **too hot**, Mama Bear's is **too cold**, Baby Bear's is **just right**.
- The same rule with the chairs: **too hard / too soft / just right**.
- What happens when Goldilocks sits on Baby Bear's chair: she **breaks it**.
- The same rule with the beds: **too hard / too soft / just right**.
- Where Goldilocks falls asleep: in **Baby Bear's bed**.
- What the bears say one after another when they return: **"Somebody has been eating my porridge / sitting in my chair / sleeping in my bed."**
- Baby Bear's last line: **"…and she's still in it!"**
- What Goldilocks does when she wakes up: she **screams and runs away**.
- Where Goldilocks goes: back **home through the woods** to her mother.
- The moral the book ends on: **listen to your mother**.
