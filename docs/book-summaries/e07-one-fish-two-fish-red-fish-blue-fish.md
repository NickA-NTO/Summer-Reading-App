# One Fish Two Fish Red Fish Blue Fish

**Author:** Dr. Seuss (Theodor Seuss Geisel)
**First published:** 1960
**Grade band:** Pre-K / Early reader
**Format:** Rhyming picture book / beginner reader

---

## Premise

A rhyming picture book without one continuous plot. The narrator (speaking sometimes as "I" and sometimes as "we") walks the reader through a parade of short rhyming scenes — first about lots of different fish, then about a long cast of made-up creatures whose names rhyme with what they do, what they wear, or what they own. The book celebrates rhyme, opposites, counting, and noticing funny things everywhere.

## Setting

A bright, made-up world. Scenes move quickly between the children's home and bedroom, a backyard, a park at night, hills where a bike has to be pushed, and open imaginary space where each new creature appears. There is no single fixed location.

## Characters

The book does not give the child narrator(s) a name. The story is told in first person, sometimes "I" and sometimes "we." The named characters that **do** appear in the text are:

- **Mr. Gump** — a man who owns a seven-hump Wump.
- **Ned** — a small character whose little bed is too short.
- **Mike** — a creature who sits in the back of a bike made for three.
- **Joe** — someone who is being telephoned.

The named creatures are:

- **The Wump** — a humped creature you can ride. The narrator's family has a Wump with **just one hump**. Mr. Gump owns a **seven-hump Wump**. Riding a Wump goes **"Bump! Bump! Bump!"**
- **The Nook** — a creature with a **hook on his head**. On the hook hangs a cookbook called **"How to Cook"**. The joke: **the Nook can't read, so the Nook can't cook.**
- **The Zans** — a creature whose job is to **open cans**. "A Zans for cans is very good."
- **The Gox** — a creature the narrator **boxes** with. The narrator wears **yellow socks** while boxing the Gox. The illustration shows the **Gox wearing yellow boxing gloves**.
- **The Ying** — a creature that **sings**.
- **The Yink** — a creature that **likes to wink** and **drinks pink ink**.
- **The Yop** — a creature that **hops from finger top to finger top**.
- **The Zeds** — small **yellow** pets with **one hair** on top of each head. The hair grows so fast that they **need a haircut every day**.
- **Ish** — a character with a **dish** in his hand; he uses it to **wish for fish**.
- **The Gack** — a creature used in a backyard game called **"Ring the Gack"**.
- **Clark** — a creature found **in the park, in the dark**, and taken home.
- **Zeep** — a pet that is taken to bed at the end of the day.

## The fish (opening section)

- **One fish, two fish, red fish, blue fish.**
- Then **black fish, blue fish, old fish, new fish.**
- One fish has **a little star**.
- Another fish has **a little car**.
- Some fish are **sad**, some are **glad**, and some are **very, very bad**.
- Some fish are **thin**, some are **fat**.
- The **fat** fish has a **yellow hat**.

## Things that happen in the book

- **A creature with eleven fingers** is shown, and the narrator points out it has **eleven**.
- The narrator rides a one-hump Wump and meets **Mr. Gump with his seven-hump Wump**.
- **Ned** doesn't like his little bed — and at one point a **cow, a dog, a cat, and a mouse** all pile into the bed with him.
- A **bird** is found inside someone's **ear**.
- **Mike** sits at the back of a **bike made for three** and does the work pushing it up the hills.
- The **Nook** is shown with his hook and his cookbook, unable to read or cook.
- By **moonlight**, sheep are seen **walking in their sleep**.
- The **Zans** opens lots and lots of **cans**.
- The narrator and the **Gox** have a boxing match — the narrator in **yellow socks**.
- The narrator **sings** along with the **Ying**.
- The **Yink** drinks **pink ink** and likes to wink.
- The **Yop** hops from one finger tip to the next.
- A pet with **blue hair** is described as **fun to brush and comb**.
- The **Zeds** (yellow, one hair, daily haircut) appear.
- A child walks past with **ten cats on her head**.
- Someone tries to phone **Joe**, but **a mouse has cut the wire**.
- **Ish** uses his **dish** to wish for fish.
- The backyard game **"Ring the Gack"** is played.
- **Clark** is found in the **park** in the **dark** and taken home.
- At bedtime the narrator sleeps with the pet **Zeep**.

## How the book ends

The book closes with a short, gentle bedtime rhyme: **"Today is gone. Today was fun. Tomorrow is another one."** It rounds off with the idea that **from here to there, funny things are everywhere**.

## Key objects and props

- **A little star** carried by one fish.
- **A little car** driven by another fish.
- **A yellow hat** on the fat fish.
- A **bike made for three**, with Mike pushing from the back.
- **A little bed** that is too small for Ned.
- **A hook** on the Nook's head, holding the cookbook **"How to Cook"**.
- **Cans** opened by the Zans.
- **Yellow socks** worn while boxing the Gox.
- **Pink ink** drunk by the Yink.
- **A dish** Ish uses to wish for fish.
- **A telephone wire** chewed through by a mouse.

## Themes and ideas

- **Imagination and inventing things** — the joy of dreaming up creatures, names, and silly situations.
- **Celebrating differences** — fish and creatures come in every shape, size, colour, mood, and habit.
- **Opposites** — old/new, sad/glad, here/there, thin/fat.
- **Counting and numbers** — one fish, two fish; one hump versus seven humps; eleven fingers; ten cats; one hair on each Zed.
- **The fun of rhyme and reading aloud** — almost every page rhymes, and rhyming pairs (Nook/cook/hook, Yink/wink/pink/drink/ink, Gox/box/socks, Zans/cans, park/dark/Clark) drive the humour.

## Tone and style

Short, bouncy rhyming couplets and quatrains. Bright cartoon illustrations. Each spread introduces a new idea, character, or joke, so children can dip in anywhere. The vocabulary is deliberately simple and repetitive so beginning readers can read it themselves.
