# Rapunzel

**Author:** Susanna Davidson (Usborne retelling of the Brothers Grimm fairy tale)
**Original tale published:** 1812 by the Brothers Grimm
**Grade band:** Grade 2 / Ages 6–9
**Format:** Illustrated retelling

---

## Premise

A husband and wife long for a baby. The wife sees a special plant called **rapunzel** (a kind of lettuce) growing in the **witch's garden** next door and craves it so badly she cannot eat. Her husband climbs over the wall to steal some, but the witch catches him. As the price for the stolen plant, she demands their **baby**. When the baby — named **Rapunzel** — is born, the witch takes her away and shuts her up in a **tall tower with no door and no stairs**, only a high window. Rapunzel grows up with **long, beautiful golden hair**. The witch climbs up to visit her by calling **"Rapunzel, Rapunzel, let down your hair!"** Years later, a **prince** discovers the tower, hears Rapunzel singing, learns the witch's trick, and climbs up to meet her. They fall in love and plan to escape. The witch finds out, **cuts off Rapunzel's hair**, and sends her away into the wilderness. The prince climbs the tower one last time, finds the witch instead, and **falls into thorns** below, which **blind him**. He wanders the world until he finally finds Rapunzel — and her **tears restore his sight**. They return to his kingdom and live happily ever after.

## Setting

A small house with a garden wall, the **witch's enchanted garden** next door, a **tall tower** in the middle of a wood, and a **wild lonely desert** where Rapunzel is exiled. Time passes from before her birth to her growing up to her marriage at the end.

## Characters

- **Rapunzel** — the girl with very long, beautiful **golden hair**. Named after the plant her mother craved. Kind, lonely, and trusting.
- **Rapunzel's father** — climbs the garden wall and is caught by the witch.
- **Rapunzel's mother** — pregnant and craving the rapunzel plant.
- **The witch (sometimes called Mother Gothel)** — the woman who owns the magic garden, takes the baby in payment, and locks her in the tower.
- **The prince** — finds the tower, falls in love with Rapunzel, and is eventually blinded.

## Plot in order

1. **A craving.** A husband and wife who have no children at last hope for a baby. The wife, looking out of the window, sees a beautiful patch of **rapunzel** (a kind of leafy lettuce) growing in the **witch's garden** next door. She wants it so much that she **stops eating**.
2. **The theft.** Her husband climbs over the high wall and steals some rapunzel for her to eat.
3. **Caught.** The next time he tries, the **witch catches him**. To save his life and his wife, he must agree to give her their **baby** as soon as it is born.
4. **The baby taken.** When the baby is born, the witch takes her away and names her **Rapunzel** after the plant. The parents never see her again.
5. **The tower.** Rapunzel grows up to be very beautiful, with extraordinarily long **golden hair**. When she is twelve, the witch shuts her in a **tall stone tower** in the middle of a wood. The tower has **no door and no stairs**, only one high window.
6. **"Let down your hair!"** Whenever the witch wants to visit, she stands at the foot of the tower and calls: **"Rapunzel, Rapunzel, let down your hair!"** Rapunzel lets her long golden braid fall out of the window all the way to the ground, and the witch **climbs up the hair**.
7. **A prince in the wood.** One day a **prince** is riding through the wood. He hears Rapunzel **singing** from her tower. He cannot find a way in.
8. **Spying the trick.** He hides nearby and one day sees the witch arrive and use the magic words. He learns the secret.
9. **A visitor.** When the witch is gone, the prince calls out **"Rapunzel, Rapunzel, let down your hair!"** Rapunzel lets her hair down. Up climbs the prince.
10. **Falling in love.** Rapunzel is startled at first but the prince is kind. They begin to fall in love and meet many times in secret.
11. **A plan to escape.** Rapunzel asks the prince to bring her a piece of silk every time he visits, so she can weave a **ladder** and climb down out of the tower.
12. **The witch finds out.** Rapunzel lets slip something that tells the witch a stranger has been visiting. The witch is furious.
13. **Hair cut off.** The witch grabs Rapunzel's beautiful hair, takes a pair of scissors, and **cuts it off**. She drags Rapunzel away and leaves her in a **wild lonely desert** to live alone.
14. **The trick on the prince.** The witch hangs the cut-off hair out of the tower window. That evening, the prince calls "Rapunzel, Rapunzel…" The hair drops. Up he climbs — and at the top he meets the **witch** instead.
15. **Into the thorns.** Terrified, the prince **jumps from the tower window**. The fall does not kill him because he lands in **thorn bushes** — but the thorns **pierce his eyes and blind him**.
16. **A long wandering.** The blind prince wanders the kingdom for **years**, sad and alone, eating wild roots and crying for his lost Rapunzel.
17. **Reunion.** Eventually he comes to the desert where Rapunzel is now living with **twin children** she has had alone. She recognises his voice. She runs to him and weeps with joy.
18. **Tears that heal.** As Rapunzel cries, **two of her tears fall on his eyes**. His **sight is restored**.
19. **Home together.** He takes Rapunzel and the children back to his kingdom. They live happily ever after.

## Key objects and props

- **The witch's garden wall** and the **rapunzel plant** growing in it.
- **The tall tower** with no door and no stairs, only one high window.
- **Rapunzel's long golden hair**, used as a ladder.
- **The magic call**: "Rapunzel, Rapunzel, let down your hair!"
- **The pieces of silk** Rapunzel was weaving into a ladder.
- **The witch's scissors** that cut off her hair.
- **The thorn bushes** that blind the prince.
- **Rapunzel's tears** that heal his eyes.

## Themes and ideas

- **A bargain too high.** Two parents give up their child for a meal. The witch's price was too steep.
- **A girl trapped by a jealous guardian.** The witch keeps Rapunzel hidden from the world.
- **The power of love.** The prince finds her, loses her, and finds her again.
- **Sacrifice and reward.** The prince suffers years of blindness; Rapunzel suffers years of exile; both are restored in the end.
- **Tears that heal.** A small magical detail — sorrow turning into healing.

## Tone and style

A short, dramatic fairy-tale retelling. The famous chant **"Rapunzel, Rapunzel, let down your hair!"** is a memorable line children love. Bright illustrations show the stone tower, the long golden braid out of the window, and the magic moment of the prince climbing up.
