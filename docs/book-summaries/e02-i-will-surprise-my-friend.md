# I Will Surprise My Friend!

**Author and Illustrator:** Mo Willems
**First published:** 2008
**Grade band:** Pre-K / Beginning reader
**Format:** Easy-reader picture book (part of the Elephant & Piggie series)

---

## Premise

**Gerald** the elephant and **Piggie** the pig see a squirrel give her squirrel friend a small surprise — and decide they want to do the same thing for each other. Each of them quietly decides to **hide behind a big rock** and **surprise the other** when they walk past. They both pick the **same big rock**. They both wait. Neither one walks past. As time goes by, each of them becomes more and more worried about the other one — Gerald imagines that Piggie has fallen off a cliff or been carried off by a giant bird, while Piggie thinks Gerald has gone home for lunch. When at last they peek around the rock and see each other, they jump in shock — and end up giving each other the **biggest surprise of all**.

## Setting

Outdoors, in a wide blank space with a few cartoon clouds, a few rocks, and not much else. The "**big rock**" that both friends hide behind is the only real piece of scenery in the book.

## Characters

- **Gerald** — the worried grey elephant. Wants to do something kind for his best friend.
- **Piggie** — the cheerful pink pig. Wants exactly the same thing.
- **Two squirrels** — appear at the start. One squirrel surprises the other with a small pebble; this is what gives Gerald and Piggie the idea.

## Plot in order

1. **The squirrel surprise.** At the beginning of the book, Gerald and Piggie see a **squirrel** sneak up and **surprise** another squirrel.
2. **A great idea.** Inspired, **Gerald** decides he wants to surprise Piggie. **Piggie**, at the same time, decides she wants to surprise Gerald.
3. **The big rock.** Each of them picks the **same big rock** to **hide behind** so they can leap out and surprise the other.
4. **Waiting.** Both of them wait on opposite sides of the rock. **Neither friend walks past**, because neither friend is anywhere else — they are both behind the rock.
5. **Worrying.** As the wait gets longer, each friend starts to worry. **Gerald imagines** Piggie has had something terrible happen — she might have **fallen off a cliff**, been carried off by **a giant bird**, or be fighting **a scary, scary monster** right now.
6. **The other side.** **Piggie**, behind her side of the rock, is also worried — though her worries are gentler. She thinks maybe Gerald has just **gone home for lunch**.
7. **The big leap.** Finally, each of them gives up waiting and steps out from behind the rock at the same moment.
8. **The biggest surprise.** They come face to face, both shouting **"SURPRISE!"** at the same instant. They jump high in the air. They have given each other the **biggest, scariest surprise** of all, because neither of them was expecting it.
9. **Best friends.** Once they recover, they laugh — happy to be together, and happy that the other one is safe.

## Key objects and props

- **The squirrels** at the beginning, whose game gives Gerald and Piggie the idea.
- **The big rock** — the centre of the whole book.
- **Gerald's thought-balloon catastrophes** — cliffs, giant birds, monsters.

## Themes and ideas

- **Surprises in friendship.** A small kind gesture turns into a big shared moment.
- **Worrying about the people you love.** When Piggie doesn't appear, Gerald's imagination immediately jumps to the worst things possible.
- **Same idea, both ends.** The joke of the book is that the two friends are doing exactly the same thing at the same time.
- **Communication.** A book about friends with such a strong bond they don't quite need to talk — but it would have helped.
- **Relief.** The funny ending is also a comfort: nothing bad happened, they're together, the day was a success.

## Tone and style

Very short text. Speech bubbles only. Lots of empty white space. Most of the humour comes from the **pictures** — Gerald slowly working himself into a panic on one side of the rock, Piggie patiently waiting on the other. The vocabulary is tiny and the sentences are short, perfect for a beginning reader.
