# Knuffle Bunny: A Cautionary Tale

**Author and Illustrator:** Mo Willems
**First published:** 2004
**Grade band:** Grade 1 / Ages 3–6
**Format:** Illustrated picture book (photographs with cartoon drawings layered on top)

---

## Premise

A toddler named **Trixie** goes on her first errand with her **daddy**, walking from home to the **laundromat**. She brings her favourite stuffed rabbit, **Knuffle Bunny**. On the way home she realises Knuffle Bunny is missing — but she **can't talk yet**, so she can't tell her father what's wrong. The book is the small, funny story of a toddler trying to make a grown-up understand something important without words, and the relief of finally getting the toy back.

## Setting

A city neighbourhood. The journey from home to the laundromat goes **down the block, through the park, past the school, to the laundromat**, and back again. The illustrations are real black-and-white photographs of the streets with cartoon drawings of Trixie, Daddy, Mommy, and Knuffle Bunny placed on top.

## Characters

- **Trixie** — a young toddler. She **cannot talk yet**, so she communicates with gestures, faces, and babbling sounds.
- **Daddy** — Trixie's father. Takes her to the laundromat as a special outing. Doesn't notice that Knuffle Bunny has been lost.
- **Mommy** — Trixie's mother. Notices immediately, the moment they walk back in the door, that Knuffle Bunny is missing.
- **Knuffle Bunny** — Trixie's stuffed rabbit toy. He is the object of the whole story. He is not a character who speaks or acts, but he is named in the title and is the focus.

No other characters are named.

## Plot in order

1. **A special errand.** Trixie goes with her daddy on an errand. They are going to the **laundromat**.
2. **Bringing Knuffle Bunny.** Trixie brings her favourite stuffed rabbit, **Knuffle Bunny**, with her.
3. **The walk there.** They walk **down the block**, **through the park**, **past the school**, and **to the laundromat**.
4. **At the laundromat.** Trixie "helps" by putting clothes into the washing machine and putting in coins. They have a good time. Trixie loves it.
5. **The walk home begins.** They leave the laundromat and start walking back home.
6. **Trixie realises.** Trixie suddenly notices something is missing. She is sure something is very wrong. **Knuffle Bunny is gone.**
7. **Trying to tell Daddy.** Trixie tries to tell her father. The only words she has are babble: she cries out **"Aggle flaggle klabble!"** Daddy looks at her cheerfully and replies that he doesn't know what she's saying.
8. **The meltdown.** Trixie keeps trying. She tries again with new sounds. Daddy still doesn't understand. So Trixie does what toddlers do — she has a tantrum. She cries. She wails. She **"goes boneless"** and flops on the pavement.
9. **Carried home.** Daddy ends up carrying a very upset Trixie home all the way **past the school, through the park, and back down the block**.
10. **Mommy notices.** The moment Daddy walks through the door with Trixie, **Mommy** takes one look and says, **"Where's Knuffle Bunny?"**
11. **Back to the laundromat.** Mommy, Daddy, and Trixie all run back to the laundromat — through all the same places — to look for Knuffle Bunny.
12. **The search.** Daddy hunts through the washing machines. He searches one after another.
13. **Found.** Daddy pulls Knuffle Bunny out of one of the machines, wet from being washed.
14. **Trixie speaks.** Trixie sees Knuffle Bunny, smiles, and shouts her very first words: **"Knuffle Bunny!"**
15. **The cautionary tale.** The book is called *A Cautionary Tale* because the warning is gentle and funny: when you can't talk yet, do **not** lose your favourite bunny on the way to the laundromat.

## Key objects and props

- **Knuffle Bunny** — Trixie's stuffed rabbit; the missing toy that drives the whole story.
- **The laundromat** — where the trouble happens. Knuffle Bunny ends up inside one of the washing machines.
- **Quarters / coins** — what Trixie helps put in the machines.
- **The washing machines** — where Daddy eventually finds Knuffle Bunny.
- **The stroller / sidewalk** — where Trixie has her boneless tantrum.
- **The route landmarks**: **block, park, school, laundromat** — repeated on the way out and on the way back.

## Themes and ideas

- **Trying to be understood.** A child has something important to say and the words won't come yet.
- **Patience and frustration.** The dad is patient and cheerful but completely wrong; the toddler is upset and absolutely right.
- **Who notices what.** Mommy spots in a second what Daddy missed completely — a small, affectionate family joke.
- **A first word.** The book ends with Trixie's first proper words. Communication has finally happened.
- **Comfort objects.** Losing a favourite toy is a real and serious thing to a small child.

## Tone and style

Very short text. Sentences are simple. The pictures are the joke: real photographs of the streets and the laundromat with cartoon characters drawn on top, so the family looks lit-up and lively against the real grey of the city. Trixie's tantrum is dramatic and funny. The book reads quickly and ends on a small triumph.
