# We're Going on a Bear Hunt

**Author:** Michael Rosen
**Illustrator:** Helen Oxenbury
**First published:** 1989
**Grade band:** Grade K / Ages 3–7
**Format:** Rhyming, chanted picture book

---

## Premise

A family — a father and his children — sets off across the countryside to find a **bear**. They chant, **"We're going on a bear hunt. We're going to catch a big one. What a beautiful day! We're not scared."** Six different landscapes get in their way, one at a time. They can't go over them. They can't go under them. They have to **go through** them. Each landscape has its own sound. At the end of their long journey, in a narrow gloomy cave, they finally come face to face with **a real bear**. They turn and run, retracing every landscape backwards, until they are safely home in bed under the covers — and they decide they will never go on a bear hunt again.

## Setting

A bright English countryside. The story moves through six very different landscapes one after the other, and then back through all six in reverse. Time is one beautiful day.

## Characters

- **A father** — leading the bear hunt.
- **His children** — the rest of the family. The book doesn't name them.
- **The family's dog** — comes along for the journey.
- **The bear** — appears only at the end, in the cave.

## The six landscapes (in order)

Each one has the same shape: "We can't go over it. We can't go under it. We've got to **go through** it!" — followed by a sound word that repeats as they cross.

1. **Long wavy grass.** Sound: **"Swishy swashy! Swishy swashy! Swishy swashy!"**
2. **A deep cold river.** Sound: **"Splash splosh! Splash splosh! Splash splosh!"**
3. **Thick oozy mud.** Sound: **"Squelch squerch! Squelch squerch! Squelch squerch!"**
4. **A big dark forest.** Sound: **"Stumble trip! Stumble trip! Stumble trip!"**
5. **A swirling, whirling snowstorm.** Sound: **"Hoooo wooooo! Hoooo wooooo! Hoooo wooooo!"**
6. **A narrow gloomy cave.** Sound: **"Tiptoe! Tiptoe! Tiptoe!"**

## Plot in order

1. **The chant.** The book opens with the family setting out, chanting: **"We're going on a bear hunt. We're going to catch a big one. What a beautiful day! We're not scared."**
2. **Through the grass.** They come to **long wavy grass**. They can't go over it. They can't go under it. They have to **go through**. **Swishy swashy.**
3. **Through the river.** They come to a **deep cold river**. **Splash splosh.**
4. **Through the mud.** They come to **thick oozy mud**. **Squelch squerch.**
5. **Through the forest.** They come to a **big dark forest**. **Stumble trip.**
6. **Through the snowstorm.** They come to a **swirling, whirling snowstorm**. **Hoooo wooooo.**
7. **Into the cave.** Finally they come to a **narrow gloomy cave**. They can't go over it or under it. They tiptoe in. **Tiptoe… tiptoe…**
8. **A bear!** In the dark of the cave they make out **one shiny wet nose, two big furry ears, two big goggly eyes** — **It's a BEAR!**
9. **Running.** They turn and **run for their lives**, back through every landscape — but this time **very fast**, in reverse:
   - back through the **cave** (tiptoe-tiptoe),
   - back through the **snowstorm** (hoooo wooooo),
   - back through the **forest** (stumble trip),
   - back through the **mud** (squelch squerch),
   - back through the **river** (splash splosh),
   - back through the **long grass** (swishy swashy),
10. **Home.** They reach their front door, run inside, up the stairs, and into bed. They **pull the covers over their heads**.
11. **Never again.** The book ends with the family decided that they are **never going on a bear hunt again**. The very last picture shows the bear walking sadly back to his cave alone.

## Key objects and props

- **The chant** — repeated again and again across the book.
- **The six landscapes** — grass, river, mud, forest, snowstorm, cave.
- **The bedroom door, stairs, and bed** — the safe place at the end.
- **The bear** — large, brown, only seen at the end. The last image shows him walking back to the cave alone.

## Themes and ideas

- **Adventure together.** The whole family goes on the hunt as a team.
- **Facing what's in front of you.** The repeated lesson: you can't go over, you can't go under, you have to go **through**.
- **Bravery and fear.** "We're not scared" is repeated until they meet the bear, and then they very much are.
- **Sounds and rhythm.** The whole book reads like a chant or a song.
- **A sympathy twist.** The very last picture quietly reminds the reader that the bear is alone again.

## Tone and style

A chanting, repeating, instantly readable picture book. Half the pages are full-colour spreads of the landscape; half are stark black-and-white scenes of the family pushing through. The text is short on each page but each scene repeats so children can quickly join in. Helen Oxenbury's illustrations are famous.
