# Charlotte's Web

**Author:** E. B. White
**Illustrator:** Garth Williams
**First published:** 1952
**Grade band:** Grade 3 / Ages 7–10
**Format:** Children's novel

---

## Premise

A friendly pig named **Wilbur**, the runt of a litter, is saved at birth by a little girl called **Fern Arable**. When Wilbur grows too big to live in the house, Fern sells him to her uncle **Homer Zuckerman's** farm, where he becomes the unlikely best friend of **Charlotte**, a clever barn spider. When Wilbur learns he is being fattened up for Christmas dinner, Charlotte decides to save him by writing words about him — **"SOME PIG"**, **"TERRIFIC"**, **"RADIANT"**, and **"HUMBLE"** — in her web. The miracle of the writing spider draws crowds, the Zuckermans take Wilbur to the **county fair**, and he wins a special prize that guarantees his life. The book ends with Charlotte's quiet death at the fairgrounds — but she leaves behind an egg sac of baby spiders that Wilbur carries home, so the friendship continues into the next generation.

## Setting

A small American farm and barn in the countryside. The two main settings are:

- **The Arable family's house and farm** — where Fern lives and where Wilbur is born.
- **Homer Zuckerman's barn** — a busy old barn with a manure pile, a doorway where Charlotte spins her web, a pasture, an apple orchard, and many animals.
- **The county fairground** — visited near the end for the final big scene.

Time covers a full year, from spring to spring.

## Characters

- **Wilbur** — a young pig. The runt of his litter. Sweet, gentle, easily upset, but capable of friendship and bravery.
- **Fern Arable** — an **eight-year-old girl**. Saves Wilbur as a piglet and feeds him with a **bottle**. As she grows up over the course of the book, she becomes more interested in another farm child (Henry Fussy) than in the animals.
- **Mr. (John) Arable** — Fern's father. Wants to kill the runt at the start; lets Fern raise him.
- **Mrs. Arable** — Fern's mother. Worries that Fern spends too much time talking to animals.
- **Avery Arable** — Fern's older brother.
- **Mr. Homer Zuckerman** — Fern's uncle, the farmer who buys Wilbur and runs the barn.
- **Mrs. Zuckerman** — his wife.
- **Lurvy** — the Zuckermans' hired hand. He brings Wilbur slop and is one of the first to spot the writing in Charlotte's web.
- **Charlotte A. Cavatica** — a grey **barn spider** with eight legs. Wise, kind, articulate. She lives in the doorway of Wilbur's pen and is Wilbur's best friend.
- **Templeton** — a **rat** who lives under Wilbur's trough. Selfish, greedy, but useful when bribed.
- **The Goose** and **the Gander** — geese in the barn; the Goose lays eggs.
- **The Sheep and the Lamb** — the elderly sheep is the one who tells Wilbur he will be killed at Christmas.
- **Uncle** — a much bigger, older pig at the county fair; Wilbur's competition.
- **Joy, Aranea, and Nellie** — three of Charlotte's daughters who stay in the barn after the others fly away.

## Plot in order

1. **A runt is born.** It is spring. Mr. Arable goes out to the barn to **kill the runt** of a new litter of pigs — because he is too small and weak.
2. **Fern saves him.** Eight-year-old **Fern** stops her father and **begs to keep the pig**. She names him **Wilbur** and feeds him from a **bottle**.
3. **Wilbur grows.** Fern raises Wilbur like a pet. As he grows, he becomes too big for the house.
4. **Sold to Uncle Zuckerman.** Mr. Arable says Wilbur must be sold. He goes to Fern's uncle, **Homer Zuckerman**, just down the road. Fern visits him every day.
5. **The Zuckerman barn.** Wilbur meets the other barn animals. The **goose**, the **sheep**, and the rat **Templeton** all live there.
6. **A new friend.** One evening, a small grey voice from a corner says: "**I'll be a friend to you.**" Wilbur soon learns the voice belongs to **Charlotte A. Cavatica**, a **barn spider** who has spun her web in the upper corner of his doorway.
7. **The terrible news.** The old sheep tells Wilbur that the Zuckermans are fattening him up to **kill him for Christmas dinner**. Wilbur is heartbroken: **"I don't want to die!"**
8. **Charlotte's promise.** Charlotte promises Wilbur she will save him. Wilbur doesn't see how a spider can save a pig.
9. **"SOME PIG."** That night Charlotte weaves the words **"SOME PIG!"** into her web above Wilbur's pen. In the morning, Lurvy sees it and the Zuckermans are amazed. The news spreads and crowds come to see the miracle.
10. **Templeton helps.** Charlotte sends **Templeton** to the **dump** to find scraps of paper with words on them so she knows new words to write.
11. **"TERRIFIC."** Charlotte weaves the word **"TERRIFIC"** into her web. More crowds come.
12. **"RADIANT."** Templeton brings the word from a **laundry-detergent advertisement**, and Charlotte writes **"RADIANT"** in her web. The story is in the newspaper.
13. **Going to the fair.** The Zuckermans decide to enter Wilbur in the **county fair**. Charlotte and Templeton come too, hidden in his crate.
14. **At the fair.** Wilbur sees a bigger pig in the next stall, an old champion called **Uncle**. Wilbur is afraid he will not win against Uncle.
15. **"HUMBLE."** Charlotte writes one last word in her web at the fair: **"HUMBLE."** It is the perfect word for Wilbur.
16. **A special prize.** Uncle wins the **blue ribbon**, but the fair organisers give Wilbur a **special prize** because of the miraculous web. The Zuckermans bring home a medal and a cash prize. They will **never kill Wilbur**.
17. **Charlotte tires.** At the fair, Charlotte is too tired and weak to spin more webs. She has spent her last strength. She quietly tells Wilbur she will not be coming home.
18. **The egg sac.** Charlotte spins one last masterpiece: a **silvery egg sac** with her **eggs** inside. Wilbur tells her he will take it home, but he needs help.
19. **Templeton's price.** Templeton finally agrees to climb up and cut down the egg sac — in return for being allowed to eat first from Wilbur's trough forever after.
20. **Charlotte dies.** Charlotte dies alone at the fairgrounds, after watching Wilbur leave with the egg sac safe in his mouth.
21. **Back at the barn.** Wilbur carries the egg sac home and keeps it warm and safe through the winter, missing Charlotte every day.
22. **Spring again.** In the spring, hundreds of **baby spiders** hatch from the egg sac. Most of them spin tiny silk balloons and **drift away on the wind** to find new homes.
23. **Three stay.** Three of Charlotte's daughters stay in the doorway: **Joy, Aranea, and Nellie**. They become Wilbur's friends.
24. **The cycle continues.** Year after year, more spiders are born and a few always stay. Wilbur lives happily on the farm, never forgetting his first and best friend, Charlotte.

## Key objects and props

- **Fern's bottle of milk** — what saves Wilbur as a piglet.
- **Charlotte's web** in the doorway of Wilbur's pen — the place where the four words appear.
- **The four words**: **SOME PIG, TERRIFIC, RADIANT, HUMBLE.**
- **Templeton's scraps** from the dump — where the new words come from.
- **The crate** Wilbur travels to the fair in.
- **The blue ribbon** (won by Uncle) and Wilbur's **special prize medal**.
- **Charlotte's egg sac** — the final masterpiece.

## Themes and ideas

- **Friendship.** The book is most of all about Charlotte and Wilbur. Their friendship saves Wilbur's life.
- **The cycle of life.** A spring of new births, a summer and autumn of growth, a winter of loss, and a new spring with new babies.
- **Loyalty and sacrifice.** Charlotte uses up her last energy to save Wilbur.
- **Growing up.** Fern starts the book talking to animals all day and ends it more interested in other children.
- **Words can change everything.** One word in a web changes Wilbur from "pork chops" to a celebrity.

## Tone and style

A warm, gentle, beautifully written children's novel. Sentences are simple but the prose is famous for being plain and beautiful at the same time. There is real sadness (a planned slaughter, Charlotte's death), but the book is never grim. Garth Williams's illustrations of the barn animals are part of the book's classic feel.
