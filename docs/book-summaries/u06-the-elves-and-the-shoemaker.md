# The Elves and the Shoemaker

**Author:** Brothers Grimm (retold for Usborne)
**First published in Grimm's collection:** Early 1800s (Kinder- und Hausmärchen)
**Grade band:** Grade 1 / Ages 5–7
**Format:** Illustrated retelling for early readers (Usborne)

---

## Premise

A poor but honest **shoemaker** and his **wife** have run out of money. He has only **enough leather for one last pair of shoes**. He cuts it out at night and goes to bed, planning to sew the shoes in the morning. When he wakes up, the shoes are **already finished, perfectly made**, sitting on his workbench. He sells them for a good price and buys leather for **two** pairs. The next morning **both pairs are done**. The same thing keeps happening — and the shoemaker and his wife slowly become rich. One night they **stay up to find out who is helping them**. They see two **tiny naked elves** at the workbench. To thank them, the wife sews them **tiny clothes**. The elves find the clothes, dance with delight, and **leave forever** — but the shoemaker is now prosperous, and he and his wife are happy.

## Setting

A small shoemaker's shop, with a workbench, a row of shoes, and a back room where the shoemaker and his wife sleep. Time covers several nights and mornings in winter.

## Characters

- **The shoemaker** — a poor, honest, hardworking craftsman. Cuts the leather and sells the shoes.
- **The shoemaker's wife** — kind and clever. She is the one who thinks of how to thank the elves.
- **Two tiny naked elves** — magical little men. They appear at midnight, make the shoes perfectly, and slip away. They never speak.

## Plot in order

1. **Poverty.** A shoemaker is so poor that he has only **enough leather left for one final pair of shoes**.
2. **Cutting at night.** That evening, the shoemaker cuts the leather into the right shapes for a pair of shoes, lays the pieces neatly on his **workbench**, and goes to bed. He plans to sew them up in the morning.
3. **A miracle.** In the morning he finds the leather **already made into a perfect pair of shoes**, sitting on the bench.
4. **A sale.** A customer comes into the shop, loves the shoes, and pays the shoemaker **more than the usual price**. Now the shoemaker has enough money to buy leather for **two pairs**.
5. **The same again.** That night he cuts the leather for two pairs, leaves it on the workbench, and goes to bed. In the morning, **two perfect pairs of shoes** are ready and waiting.
6. **Rich and richer.** This pattern continues night after night. Whatever leather the shoemaker cuts, **finished shoes** are on the bench the next morning. The shoes are so beautifully made that they sell quickly and for high prices. The shoemaker and his wife are no longer poor.
7. **The plan to stay up.** One night near Christmas, the shoemaker and his wife decide they must find out who is helping them. They **hide in the corner of the workshop** behind a curtain to watch.
8. **The two little men.** At midnight, **two tiny naked men** — elves — quietly come in. They sit down at the workbench and begin to sew shoes with **amazing speed and skill**, never speaking, never stopping. They finish all the shoes and slip away into the night.
9. **A kind plan.** The shoemaker and his wife are amazed. The wife notices that the little men have **no clothes** and must be cold. To thank them, she decides to **sew them tiny clothes**: little **shirts, jackets, trousers, and stockings**. The shoemaker makes each of them a tiny pair of **shoes**.
10. **A surprise on the workbench.** That night, instead of leaving out leather, the shoemaker and his wife leave out the **little clothes** they have made.
11. **The elves return.** At midnight the two elves arrive. They see the tiny clothes. They put them on, **dance** around the workshop in delight, **sing**, and skip away out of the door.
12. **The elves never come back.** From that night on, the elves do not return. But the shoemaker has learned his trade and now has plenty of money. He and his wife **live happily ever after**, prospering in everything they do.

## Key objects and props

- **The very last piece of leather** at the start of the story.
- **The workbench** in the shop.
- **The first perfect pair of shoes** made overnight.
- **The growing pile of money** the shoemaker earns from his sales.
- **The curtain** the shoemaker and his wife hide behind.
- **The tiny clothes** the wife sews: **little shirts, jackets, trousers, and stockings**, plus tiny **shoes**.

## Themes and ideas

- **Kindness is repaid.** Small, magical strangers help a kind, honest shoemaker, and a kind shoemaker thanks them in return.
- **Gratitude.** The wife's idea of sewing the elves clothes is the heart of the tale.
- **Hard work and luck.** The shoemaker works honestly; the elves help; together they make a good life.
- **Helpers who go away.** The elves give their gift, are thanked, and disappear — they were never meant to stay.
- **Magic in everyday work.** A workshop, a hammer, and some leather become the setting for a small miracle.

## Tone and style

A short, warm, fairy-tale retelling. The pattern repeats — cutting at night, finished shoes in the morning — which gives the story its rhythm. Bright illustrations show the dark workshop, the two tiny elves at the bench, and the moment they dance away in their new clothes.
