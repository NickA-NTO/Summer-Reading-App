# The Very Hungry Caterpillar

**Author and Illustrator:** Eric Carle
**First published:** 1969
**Grade band:** Grade K / Ages 2–5
**Format:** Short illustrated picture book (with cut-out holes the caterpillar has "eaten" through the pages)

---

## Premise

A small **caterpillar** hatches from an egg on a leaf. He is very, very hungry. Over the course of a week he eats through more and more food — first one apple, then two pears, then a long list of foods on Saturday that gives him a stomachache. After eating one nice green leaf to feel better, he builds himself a **cocoon**, sleeps inside for over two weeks, and finally emerges as a beautiful **butterfly**.

## Setting

A leafy outdoor world. The book moves quickly across **seven days of the week**, from one night when an egg sits on a leaf, through a week of eating, to the day the caterpillar emerges from his cocoon as a butterfly.

## Characters

- **The caterpillar** — the only real character. He hatches small and green, eats his way through the week, and changes into a butterfly.
- **The egg, the cocoon, and the butterfly** — three stages of the same animal.

No other named characters.

## Plot in order

1. **The egg.** One night, a **tiny egg** lies on a **leaf**.
2. **Sunday morning.** On Sunday morning the sun comes up, the egg cracks, and out comes a **tiny and very hungry caterpillar**.
3. **Monday.** On Monday, the caterpillar eats through **one apple**. But he is still hungry.
4. **Tuesday.** On Tuesday, he eats through **two pears**. Still hungry.
5. **Wednesday.** On Wednesday, **three plums**. Still hungry.
6. **Thursday.** On Thursday, **four strawberries**. Still hungry.
7. **Friday.** On Friday, **five oranges**. Still hungry.
8. **Saturday.** On Saturday the caterpillar really lets himself go and eats:
   - one piece of **chocolate cake**,
   - one **ice-cream cone**,
   - one **pickle**,
   - one slice of **Swiss cheese**,
   - one slice of **salami**,
   - one **lollipop**,
   - one piece of **cherry pie**,
   - one **sausage**,
   - one **cupcake**, and
   - one slice of **watermelon**.
9. **A stomachache.** That night the caterpillar has a **stomachache** from eating so much.
10. **Sunday again.** On the next Sunday, the caterpillar eats through **one nice green leaf**. He feels much better.
11. **Big and fat.** The caterpillar is now a **big, fat caterpillar** — no longer little or hungry.
12. **The cocoon.** The big caterpillar builds himself a small **house called a cocoon** around himself.
13. **Two weeks.** He stays inside for **more than two weeks**.
14. **A butterfly.** Then he **nibbles a hole** in the cocoon, pushes his way out, and is now a beautiful, brightly coloured **butterfly**.

## Key objects and props

- **The egg** on the leaf.
- **The food the caterpillar eats** on every day of the week — each item is shown with a **little hole** cut through the page where he has eaten.
- **The big leaf** that finally settles his stomach.
- **The cocoon** he builds for himself.
- **The butterfly** at the end.

## Themes and ideas

- **The life cycle of a butterfly.** Egg → caterpillar → cocoon → butterfly, told as a story.
- **Days of the week.** The book teaches the names of the seven days in order.
- **Counting.** One apple, two pears, three plums, four strawberries, five oranges.
- **Healthy and unhealthy food.** Saturday's feast gives the caterpillar a stomachache. One green leaf makes him feel better.
- **Growing up.** A small hungry creature becomes a big beautiful one.

## Tone and style

A short, repetitive, brilliantly illustrated picture book in Eric Carle's signature **collage style** (painted tissue paper). The pages have actual **die-cut holes** through them where the caterpillar has "eaten" his way through the food. Sentences are short and the structure repeats so children can predict and join in.
