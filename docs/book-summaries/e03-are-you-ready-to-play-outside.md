# Are You Ready to Play Outside?

**Author and Illustrator:** Mo Willems
**First published:** 2008
**Grade band:** Pre-K / Beginning reader
**Format:** Easy-reader picture book (part of the Elephant & Piggie series)

---

## Premise

**Piggie** is desperate to play outside in the sunshine. **Gerald** the elephant joins her. They start running and jumping happily — and then it starts to **rain**. Piggie is devastated. She thinks the day is ruined. Then two cheerful **worms** wander past, splashing and playing in the puddles. The worms show Gerald and Piggie that the rain isn't a problem — it is actually a lot of fun. The two friends start to play in the rain too. As soon as they have learned to love the rain, the rain **stops**. Now Piggie is upset again. The book ends with a joke about whatever the weather does, Gerald and Piggie will be playing outside together.

## Setting

Outdoors, on a sunny day that quickly turns into a rainy day and then into a clearing one. The book uses very simple backgrounds — sun, clouds, raindrops, puddles — to mark the weather changes.

## Characters

- **Piggie** — the pink pig. Cheerful at first, dramatically upset when the rain comes.
- **Gerald** — the grey elephant. Patient and steady. His big ear comes in useful as a kind of umbrella over Piggie.
- **Two worms** — pink, smiling, completely unbothered by the rain. They give Gerald and Piggie the idea to keep playing.

## Plot in order

1. **Sunshine.** Piggie is full of energy: she **wants to play outside**. She runs, jumps, and is full of joy.
2. **Gerald comes along.** Gerald says yes and joins her. They are having a great time in the sunshine.
3. **Raindrops.** A few **raindrops** begin to fall. Piggie can hardly believe it.
4. **Piggie melts down.** The rain gets heavier. Piggie is heartbroken. Her perfect day outside has been ruined.
5. **Gerald's ear.** Gerald, kind as ever, uses his **big ear as an umbrella** to keep some of the rain off Piggie.
6. **The worms.** Two small **worms** wriggle past, playing in the rain. They are not upset by the rain at all — they love it. They run, they splash in **puddles**, they laugh.
7. **A change of heart.** Gerald and Piggie watch the worms. They realise that the rain isn't ruining anything — they can just play in it instead.
8. **Playing in the rain.** Gerald and Piggie **splash and dance** in the rain. Piggie has never had so much fun.
9. **The rain stops.** Just when they are loving the rain, it **stops raining**. The sun comes back out.
10. **A second meltdown.** Piggie is upset again — she had only just started to love the rain.
11. **A friendly lesson.** Gerald reminds Piggie that you can be happy whatever the weather, as long as you are with your best friend.
12. **Playing outside.** The book ends with Gerald and Piggie back in the sun, still playing outside together.

## Key objects and props

- **The sun** at the start of the book.
- **The raindrops** and **puddles** in the middle.
- **Gerald's big ear** used as an umbrella.
- **The two cheerful worms** splashing in the rain.

## Themes and ideas

- **Things don't always go to plan.** The day doesn't go the way Piggie expected — and that's okay.
- **Friends help each other through.** Gerald is patient with Piggie's drama.
- **Joy comes from how you look at it.** The rain only ruins things if you let it.
- **Learning from someone new.** Two random worms, just by being themselves, change Piggie's day.
- **Whatever the weather, together.** The book ends on the idea that the company matters more than the weather.

## Tone and style

Short text. Mostly speech bubbles. Big, expressive cartoon faces show Piggie's emotions sweeping from joy to despair to joy again. Lots of white space, and minimal background — just enough to show whether it's sunny, raining, or clearing up.
