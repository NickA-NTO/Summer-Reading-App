# The Dot

**Author and Illustrator:** Peter H. Reynolds
**First published:** 2003
**Grade band:** Grade 1 / Ages 5–8
**Format:** Short illustrated picture book

---

## Premise

A short picture book about a girl named **Vashti** who is sure she cannot draw. Her art teacher gently encourages her to **"just make a mark and see where it takes you."** Vashti jabs a single dot on the page. From that one dot, with the teacher's quiet encouragement, Vashti grows into a real artist — and at the end she passes the same lesson on to another child who thinks he cannot draw either. The book is about creative courage, encouragement, and how the smallest start can lead to something big.

## Setting

Vashti's school — specifically her **art class**, where the story opens with her sitting at an empty desk staring at a blank piece of paper. The story later moves to other places in the school: the art room where her framed dot hangs, the wider school for the art show.

## Characters

- **Vashti** — the main character. A girl who believes at first that she cannot draw. Through the book she becomes a confident artist.
- **The art teacher** — never named. A patient, calm teacher whose simple words ("Just make a mark…", "Now sign it") drive the whole story.
- **A little boy** — appears at the end. He admires Vashti's art and tells her he cannot draw either. He becomes the next "Vashti" in the story's quiet cycle.

No other characters are named.

## Plot in order

1. **Art class is over.** Everyone has finished. Vashti is still sitting at her desk with a **blank sheet of paper** in front of her.
2. **"I just can't draw!"** Vashti tells her teacher she cannot draw.
3. **The teacher smiles.** Instead of arguing, her teacher says: **"Just make a mark and see where it takes you."**
4. **The dot.** Vashti grabs a marker and **jabs at the paper**, making a **small, sharp dot**.
5. **"Now sign it."** The teacher pushes the paper back towards her and quietly says: **"Now sign it."** Vashti thinks this is silly, but she signs her name in the corner.
6. **The framed dot.** The following week, Vashti comes back to art class and sees her own little signed dot **hanging in a gold frame above the teacher's desk**.
7. **"I can make a better dot than that!"** Looking at her framed dot, Vashti decides she could do better. She paints another dot.
8. **More dots.** Vashti starts experimenting. She paints **lots of dots** — small dots, bigger dots, dots in **many colours** (red, yellow, blue, green), dots that overlap to make purple, even **huge dots** painted with big swipes.
9. **A dot without a dot.** Vashti makes one painting that is just a big circle of colour with the dot in the middle being the empty space — a **dot made by NOT painting a dot**.
10. **The school art show.** Vashti's many dot paintings are displayed at a school art show. There are dots of all sizes and colours — a whole wall of them.
11. **A young admirer.** A **little boy** comes up to her at the show and says he wishes he could draw like her. He tells her he can't even draw a straight line with a ruler.
12. **Vashti's turn to teach.** Vashti hands him a **blank sheet of paper** and tells him to show her a line.
13. **The boy's mark.** With a **shaky, trembling pencil**, the boy draws a **wobbly line** and hands the paper back.
14. **"Please… sign it."** Vashti looks at his line, then hands it back to him and says: **"Please… sign it."** The story ends there, with the cycle of encouragement passed on.

## Key objects and props

- **The blank sheet of paper** at the start (and again at the end).
- **The marker / pen** that makes Vashti's first dot.
- **The single small dot** — the seed of the whole story.
- **The gold frame** that hangs Vashti's signed dot above the teacher's desk.
- **Vashti's many later dot paintings** — small, large, multi-coloured, overlapping, and the "dot without a dot."
- **The boy's wobbly line** at the end — his first mark.

## Themes and ideas

- **Creative courage.** Believing you can't do something stops you from starting. The book is about taking the first step.
- **Encouragement from a teacher.** The teacher's two simple lines — "Just make a mark and see where it takes you" and "Now sign it" — change everything.
- **Signing your work.** Putting your name on what you've made turns a mark into art and gives you ownership.
- **Passing it on.** What Vashti is given by her teacher, she gives to the next child. Confidence spreads.
- **Small beginnings, big results.** One tiny dot becomes a whole art show.

## Tone and style

Very short text, lots of white space, gentle watercolour-and-ink illustrations. The book is meant to be read in one sitting. Most of the "action" is internal: a child's feeling of "I can't" turning into "I can." The tone is calm, warm, and quietly inspirational.
