# Fantastic Mr. Fox

**Author:** Roald Dahl
**Illustrator (original):** Donald Chaffin (later editions illustrated by Quentin Blake)
**First published:** 1970
**Grade band:** Grade 2 / Ages 7–10
**Format:** Short illustrated chapter book

---

## Premise

Three cruel, rich farmers — **Boggis**, **Bunce**, and **Bean** — own the three farms in the valley. Every night, the clever **Mr. Fox** sneaks out of his hole under a tree and steals food for his family from one of their farms. Furious, the three farmers stake out his hole with shotguns and start digging him out, first with spades and then with great machines. Mr. Fox and his family escape by digging deeper and deeper underground, and Mr. Fox comes up with a brilliant plan: tunnel straight into each farmer's storeroom and feast on everything inside. The book ends with all the animals having a great underground banquet while the three farmers sit at the entrance of the hole, waiting for a fox who will never come out.

## Setting

A small valley with **three farms**. Mr. Fox and his family live in a **hole under a tree** on a hill overlooking the three farms. The story takes place above ground in fields and orchards, and then almost entirely underground in tunnels Mr. Fox digs from his home all the way to each farmer's storeroom: **Boggis's chicken houses**, **Bunce's storehouse**, and **Bean's secret cider cellar**.

## Characters

- **Mr. Fox** — the hero. Clever, calm, kind to his family, and the best thief in the valley.
- **Mrs. Fox** — Mr. Fox's wife. Calm, brave, and proud of her husband.
- **The four Small Foxes** — Mr. and Mrs. Fox's four young children. They are not given names in the book.
- **Boggis** — the **chicken farmer**. "Enormously fat." Eats **three boiled chickens smothered with dumplings for breakfast, lunch, and supper**.
- **Bunce** — the **duck-and-goose farmer**. "A kind of pot-bellied dwarf." So short his chin would be underwater in the shallow end of any swimming pool. Lives on **doughnuts and goose-livers**.
- **Bean** — the **turkey-and-apple farmer**. **"Thin as a pencil"** and the **cleverest** of the three. He never eats — he just drinks **gallons of strong cider** made from the apples in his orchard.
- **Badger** — a friendly badger who lives nearby. Joins the foxes underground.
- **Mrs. Badger and Badger's children** — Badger's family.
- **The Moles, the Rabbits, and the Weasels** — other animals driven from their homes by the farmers' digging, who all end up underground with Mr. Fox.

## Plot in order

1. **The three farmers.** The book opens with a description of the three nasty farmers and the famous rhyme:
   > Boggis and Bunce and Bean
   > One fat, one short, one lean.
   > These horrible crooks,
   > So different in looks,
   > Were none the less equally mean.
2. **Where Mr. Fox lives.** Mr. Fox, Mrs. Fox, and their four small children live in a snug hole under a great tree on a hill, looking down at the three farms.
3. **Mr. Fox's nightly raids.** Every evening, Mr. Fox asks Mrs. Fox what they would like for supper. Then he sneaks down the hill in the dark and steals **chickens, ducks, geese, or turkeys** from one of the three farms — taking it in turns to keep them guessing.
4. **The farmers plot together.** Boggis, Bunce, and Bean meet to plan how to catch the fox. **Bean** comes up with the plan: they will lie in wait outside the fox's hole at night with **shotguns**.
5. **The first ambush.** The three farmers crouch in the dark with their guns. When Mr. Fox pokes his nose out of the hole, they fire — and **shoot off his tail**. Mr. Fox dives back inside.
6. **The dig with spades.** Furious, the three farmers come back the next day with **shovels and spades** and start to dig the fox out. Mr. Fox tells his family to dig **down**, fast.
7. **The dig with machines.** When the spades aren't fast enough, Bean brings in two huge **mechanical diggers (excavators)**. They tear the top off the hill, lop the tree off at the trunk, and dig a vast crater.
8. **Foxes underground.** Mr. Fox and his family keep digging downwards, faster than the machines can follow.
9. **A standoff.** Realising they cannot dig deep enough to reach the foxes, Boggis, Bunce, and Bean decide to **starve them out**. They surround the hole and sit in the field above with their shotguns, ready for the foxes to come up.
10. **Three days underground.** The fox family spends **three days** trapped underground with nothing to eat. The cubs grow weaker and weaker. Mrs. Fox is brave but exhausted.
11. **A plan.** On the third day, Mr. Fox has his idea: instead of going **up** for food, they will dig **sideways**. He sets out with the strongest of his sons.
12. **Boggis's Chicken House Number One.** Mr. Fox and his son dig straight into **Boggis's giant chicken house**. They take three of the plumpest hens back to Mrs. Fox.
13. **Meeting Badger.** Mr. Fox meets **Badger**, who has been dug out of his own home by the farmers. Badger and his family — and the Moles, the Rabbits, and the Weasels — are all hiding underground, starving too. Mr. Fox invites them to join him.
14. **Bunce's Mighty Storehouse.** Together they dig through to **Bunce's storehouse**, where Bunce keeps **smoked hams, slabs of bacon, ducks, geese, jars of carrots, and jars of pickles**. They take whatever they need.
15. **Bean's Secret Cider Cellar.** Then they dig through to **Bean's secret cider cellar**, where great jars of golden cider are kept. In the cellar they nearly get caught by **Mabel**, a maid sent down for a jug of cider, but they hide and she leaves.
16. **The banquet.** Mrs. Fox prepares a great underground **celebration banquet** with all the food they have stolen. All the animals — Foxes, Badgers, Moles, Rabbits, and Weasels — sit down together. Mr. Fox stands up and toasts the meal.
17. **The new underground neighbourhood.** Mr. Fox announces that from now on, the animals will live underground together, with tunnels back to each farm whenever they need food. They will never have to leave their burrows again.
18. **The farmers above.** Up in the field, Boggis, Bunce, and Bean are still **sitting in the rain** with their shotguns, watching the fox hole. They have no idea that the foxes have already left.
19. **The closing line.** The book ends with the narrator telling us: **"And so far as I know, they are still waiting."**

## Key objects and props

- **Mr. Fox's tail** — shot off in the first ambush.
- **The shotguns** Boggis, Bunce, and Bean use.
- **The two mechanical diggers (excavators)** that destroy the top of the hill.
- **The three tunnels** Mr. Fox digs — to Boggis's chicken house, Bunce's storehouse, and Bean's cider cellar.
- **Boggis's chickens** — the first food.
- **Bunce's smoked hams, ducks, geese, and pickles** — the second.
- **Bean's golden cider** — the third.
- **The great underground banquet** at the end.

## Themes and ideas

- **Cleverness beats cruelty.** The farmers have guns, machines, and money; Mr. Fox has only brains and patience. He wins.
- **Family and community.** Mr. Fox shares everything with Mrs. Fox, his cubs, Badger, and all the other displaced animals.
- **Bullies and underdogs.** The three farmers are described as horrible from the very first page. The reader is always on the foxes' side.
- **Resourcefulness in trouble.** When the situation looks hopeless (starving, surrounded, no tail), Mr. Fox just digs harder.
- **The grown-ups are wrong.** A very Roald Dahl idea — the cruel grown-ups never realise they have been outsmarted.

## Tone and style

Short, fast, very funny chapters. Roald Dahl's narrator is a quiet jokester, gently in love with the foxes and openly disgusted by the farmers. Vocabulary is rich but the sentences are short. There are silly rhymes, vivid descriptions, and lots of named foods. The book sits between picture-book and novel — perfect for a child moving up to chapter books.
