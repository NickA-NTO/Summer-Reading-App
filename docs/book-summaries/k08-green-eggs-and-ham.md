# Green Eggs and Ham

**Author and Illustrator:** Dr. Seuss (Theodor Seuss Geisel)
**First published:** 1960
**Grade band:** Grade K / Ages 4–7
**Format:** Rhyming picture book / beginning reader (Beginner Books series)

---

## Premise

A cheerful little creature called **Sam-I-am** desperately wants a grumpy unnamed character to try a dish of **green eggs and ham**. The character refuses again and again, in every place and with every companion Sam-I-am can think of — in a house, with a mouse, in a box, with a fox, on a train, in a tree, in the rain, in the dark, on a boat, with a goat. Each refusal is louder than the last. Finally, just to make Sam-I-am stop pestering him, the grumpy character agrees to try **one bite** — and discovers he **loves** green eggs and ham. He thanks Sam-I-am and will now eat them anywhere, with anyone.

## Setting

A bright Dr. Seuss world that keeps changing. Each new place Sam-I-am suggests the character try green eggs and ham becomes its own quick scene: a tiny house with a mouse, a box on a fox's head, a tree (with a goat), a train, a moving train in the dark and the rain, a small boat tipping into the sea. The whole book happens in less than a single day — a chase from place to place.

## Characters

- **Sam-I-am** — small, smiley, polite, and absolutely **persistent**. Always carries a plate of green eggs and ham. Wears a tall red hat, and at the start rides past showing a sign that says **"I am Sam"**.
- **The unnamed grumpy character** — a larger Seuss creature with a long face and a long red hat. Never named; he is just "I."
- **A mouse** — appears in the "in a house, with a mouse" scenes.
- **A fox** — appears in the "with a fox / in a box" scenes.
- **A goat** — appears in the "with a goat / on a boat" scenes.

## Plot in order

1. **Sam-I-am.** The narrator declares he does not like **Sam-I-am**. Sam-I-am keeps trying to introduce himself.
2. **The first offer.** Sam-I-am asks: **"Do you like green eggs and ham?"** The character says **"I do not like them, Sam-I-am. I do not like green eggs and ham."**
3. **In a house? With a mouse?** Sam-I-am asks if he would eat them **in a house** or **with a mouse**. The grumpy character refuses: not in a house, not with a mouse.
4. **In a box? With a fox?** Sam-I-am offers them **in a box** or **with a fox**. Still no. Not in a box, not with a fox. Not in a house, not with a mouse.
5. **In a tree.** Sam-I-am tries **in a tree**. Still no.
6. **On a train! On a train!** Sam-I-am races a train past him offering green eggs and ham. No, no, no.
7. **In the dark.** Sam-I-am turns off the lights and offers them **in the dark**. No.
8. **In the rain.** Then **in the rain**. Still no.
9. **With a goat.** Sam-I-am brings out a **goat** carrying the plate. Still no.
10. **On a boat.** They all end up on a small **boat**: Sam-I-am, the goat, the fox, the mouse, the grumpy character, the train, and the plate of green eggs and ham. The boat is overloaded and tips over.
11. **Soaked and surrounded.** Bobbing in the water with everyone, the grumpy character finally gives in. He says he will try them **just to make Sam-I-am stop asking**.
12. **One bite.** He tastes the green eggs and ham.
13. **A change of heart.** It turns out **he likes them. He likes them, Sam-I-am!**
14. **Will eat anywhere.** Now that he likes them, he announces he **will** eat them in a house, with a mouse, in a box, with a fox, in a tree, in a train, in the dark, in the rain, with a goat, on a boat — all the places he refused.
15. **Thank you.** He thanks Sam-I-am for his patience. **"Thank you! Thank you, Sam-I-am!"**

## Key objects and props

- **The plate of green eggs and ham** — Sam-I-am carries it everywhere.
- **The "I am Sam" sign** Sam-I-am shows at the start.
- **The box** that the character won't eat in.
- **The tree** that the character won't eat in.
- **The train** that races by.
- **The boat** that everyone ends up on.
- **The water** they all fall into when the boat tips.

## Themes and ideas

- **Trying new things.** The lesson is right on the surface: you may not know you like something until you try it.
- **Patience and persistence.** Sam-I-am never gets discouraged. He just keeps offering.
- **Saying no, then saying yes.** The "no's" get bigger and louder before the eventual "yes."
- **Friendship after disagreement.** By the end, the grumpy character not only enjoys the food but **thanks Sam-I-am**.
- **Repetition and word families.** The book uses only about fifty different words; **house/mouse, box/fox, train/rain, goat/boat, tree/me, here/there** drive the whole rhyme.

## Tone and style

Hugely repetitive, hugely rhyming, hugely satisfying. The book famously uses about fifty distinct words — Dr. Seuss wrote it on a bet that he couldn't make an engaging picture book with so few. Each spread shows another place to refuse green eggs and ham, drawn in bright, simple Seuss style.

## Quick fact bank (useful for question writing)

- The persistent character with the plate of food: **Sam-I-am**.
- What food Sam-I-am keeps offering: **green eggs and ham**.
- How the grumpy character feels about it at first: he **does not like green eggs and ham**.
- The first place Sam-I-am suggests he eats them: **in a house, with a mouse**.
- Other places and companions Sam-I-am suggests:
  - **in a box, with a fox**,
  - **here or there**,
  - **in a tree**,
  - **in a train**,
  - **in the dark**,
  - **in the rain**,
  - **with a goat**,
  - **on a boat**.
- What happens to everyone on the boat: it **tips over** and they all fall in the water.
- Why the grumpy character finally tries them: just to make **Sam-I-am stop asking** (a "you let me be" deal).
- How the grumpy character feels after he tastes them: he **likes them!**
- What he now says he will eat them with: **a fox, a mouse, a goat, on a train, in a tree, in a box, in the dark, in the rain, on a boat, in a house** — anywhere.
- What he says to Sam-I-am at the end: **"Thank you! Thank you, Sam-I-am!"**
- A famous fact about the book: it is written using only about **fifty different words**.
- The big idea of the book: **try it before you say you don't like it**.
