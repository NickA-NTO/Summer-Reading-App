# Junie B. Jones and the Stupid Smelly Bus

**Author:** Barbara Park
**Illustrator:** Denise Brunkus
**First published:** 1992
**Grade band:** Grade 3 / Ages 6–9
**Format:** Early chapter book (first book in the Junie B. Jones series)

---

## Premise

The very first book in the Junie B. Jones series. **Junie B. Jones** — a feisty, loud, very funny girl who insists on being called Junie B. ("the B is just the letter B, I like it") — is about to start **kindergarten**. Her mother says she has to ride **the bus** to and from school. After a bumpy bus ride to school and a long first day, Junie B. hears a rumour that **bigger kids pour chocolate milk on your head on the bus home**. Terrified, she decides not to get on the bus. Instead she **hides in the school supply closet**, gets locked inside the empty school, has a series of small disasters, and eventually has to be rescued. The book ends with her making a new friend named **Grace** and deciding maybe the bus ride home won't be so bad after all.

## Setting

A small American town. Almost the entire book takes place at **Junie B.'s school**, especially Room Nine (her kindergarten classroom), the school bus, the supply closet, and the after-hours hallways, library, and nurse's office. The opening and closing scenes are at Junie B.'s house with her mother.

## Characters

- **Junie B. Jones** — the loud, funny, opinionated narrator. About to start kindergarten. She insists that the **B is just the letter B** because she likes it. She is bossy, brave, and not great at sharing.
- **Junie B.'s mother** — the parent in the book. She makes Junie B. take the bus and drives over in panic when Junie B. is missing.
- **Junie B.'s teacher** — addressed only as **"Mrs."** Junie B. cannot pronounce her name properly, so she just calls her "Mrs."
- **Jim** — a mean little boy on the bus. He **yells at Junie B.**, won't let her touch his backpack, and **steps on her shoe**.
- **Lucille** — a fancy little girl in Junie B.'s class. She is the one who tells Junie B. the **chocolate-milk rumour**. Junie B. likes her clothes but she is not really kind.
- **Grace** — a kind girl on the bus and in Junie B.'s class. By the end of the book she becomes Junie B.'s **new friend**.
- **The school principal** — arrives during the rescue at the end.
- **The janitor / first responders** — paramedics, firefighters, and police officers all turn up at the school after Junie B. calls 911.

## Plot in order

1. **First day jitters.** Junie B. Jones is starting **kindergarten** today. She really, really does **not** want to ride the **stupid smelly bus**.
2. **On the bus to school.** Junie B. is forced onto the bus. She sits next to a boy named **Jim**. Jim yells at her, won't let her touch his backpack, and steps on her shoe.
3. **At school.** Junie B. arrives at her kindergarten classroom, **Room Nine**. Her teacher is **Mrs.** — Junie B. can't say the full name and so just calls her "Mrs."
4. **Meeting Lucille.** Junie B. is impressed by **Lucille**, a girl in her class who wears fancy clothes.
5. **A long first day.** Junie B. has school activities, snack time, and lunchtime. She is loud, mostly enthusiastic, and gets into small scrapes.
6. **The chocolate-milk rumour.** Later in the day, **Lucille** tells Junie B. that on the bus home, the **bigger kids pour chocolate milk on the smaller kids' heads**.
7. **A horrible thought.** Junie B. is now terrified of the ride home. She decides she is not getting on that bus, no matter what.
8. **A clever plan.** When the bell rings at the end of the day, Junie B. **sneaks out of line** and **hides in the school supply closet** in Room Nine.
9. **Locked inside.** She hears the bus drive away. She hears the other children leave. Eventually the school goes quiet, and Junie B. realises she is **locked inside the empty school**.
10. **Exploring.** Junie B. comes out of the closet and **explores** the empty school. She goes through the **hallways**, peeks into the **library**, and ends up in the **nurse's office**.
11. **In the nurse's office.** She tries on the nurse's things, eats some crackers, and starts to feel scared and lonely.
12. **Calling for help.** She picks up the phone and **calls 911** for help.
13. **A big rescue.** Soon the school is full of grown-ups: **police officers, firefighters, paramedics**, the **principal**, and finally her very worried **mother**.
14. **Mother and child.** Junie B.'s mother is not really angry, just relieved. Junie B. tells her about Jim, about Lucille, and about the chocolate-milk rumour.
15. **A new friend.** Back at home, Junie B. talks on the phone to **Grace**, a girl from her class who also rides the bus. Grace is **kind**, and she tells Junie B. that the chocolate-milk story is **not true**.
16. **The next morning.** With Grace as her seatmate, Junie B. now thinks the bus might be okay. The book ends with her ready (sort of) to get on the bus the next day with her new friend.

## Key objects and props

- **The stupid smelly bus** — the bus Junie B. dreads.
- **The school supply closet** in Room Nine — where Junie B. hides.
- **The chocolate milk** — the imagined punishment of the bus ride.
- **The nurse's office** — where Junie B. eats crackers and finally uses the phone.
- **The telephone** Junie B. uses to call 911.
- **Junie B.'s grown-up rescue crew** — the police, fire crew, paramedics, principal, and her mother.

## Themes and ideas

- **Starting school.** Almost everything in the book is a small "first day" worry blown up into something funny and big.
- **Mishearing the world.** Junie B. believes the chocolate-milk story completely. Children believe what bigger kids tell them.
- **Bravery and panic.** Junie B. hides because she is scared — but she's also brave enough to call for help when she is alone.
- **Finding the right friend.** Lucille is glamorous but not really kind. Grace is the better choice.
- **Grown-ups making things okay.** When Junie B.'s mother shows up, the panic ends.

## Tone and style

A short, hilarious chapter book written entirely in **Junie B.'s own voice**, in first person. Her grammar is famously messy ("the bestest day of my life"), which is the joke. Sentences are short, with lots of capital letters and ALL CAPS for emphasis. Chapters are very short. Denise Brunkus's pen-and-ink illustrations of a wild-haired Junie B. are scattered throughout.
