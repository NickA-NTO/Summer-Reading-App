# Pinocchio

**Author:** Carlo Collodi (retold for Usborne)
**First published:** Serialised in Italian 1881–1883; book form 1883 (as *Le avventure di Pinocchio*)
**Grade band:** Grade 2 / Ages 6–9
**Format:** Illustrated retelling for early readers (Usborne)

---

## Premise

A poor old **woodcarver** named **Geppetto** carves a wooden **puppet boy** out of a magical piece of wood. To his amazement, the puppet **comes to life** and begins to talk and walk by himself. Geppetto names him **Pinocchio**. Pinocchio is meant to bring joy to Geppetto's lonely home — but the moment he can run, he runs away, and gets into one piece of trouble after another. Throughout his adventures Pinocchio is helped by a kind **Blue Fairy** and warned by a wise **talking cricket**, but tricked by a sly **Fox and Cat**. He learns the hard way that **his nose grows when he tells lies**, that bad friends turn boys into **donkeys**, and that being swallowed by a giant fish is no fun. After many troubles he saves his father from inside the giant fish's belly, behaves bravely and kindly at last, and is rewarded by the Fairy — who turns him into a **real boy**.

## Setting

A small **Tuscan village** in Italy and the countryside around it. Pinocchio's adventures travel from Geppetto's tiny workshop, through a forest, to a strange country (called the **Land of Toys / Toyland** in many retellings), to a school, and finally into the belly of a **giant fish (a great shark / whale)** in the sea.

## Characters

- **Pinocchio** — the wooden puppet boy. Carved by Geppetto and brought to life. Mischievous, impulsive, and easily led — but in the end, brave and loving.
- **Geppetto** — a poor old woodcarver. Lonely. He treats Pinocchio like his own son. He spends most of the book searching for him.
- **The Talking Cricket** — a wise old cricket who lives in Geppetto's house and tries to warn Pinocchio against bad behaviour. (The model for Disney's "Jiminy Cricket.")
- **The Blue Fairy** — also called the Fairy with Turquoise Hair. A magical, kind woman who watches over Pinocchio and grants his final wish.
- **The Fox and the Cat** — two sly con-men. The Fox pretends to be lame; the Cat pretends to be blind. They trick Pinocchio out of his gold coins.
- **The Coachman / driver to Toyland** — who lures bad boys away to a holiday country and turns them into donkeys.
- **The Terrible Shark / giant fish** — an enormous sea creature that swallows Pinocchio and Geppetto.

## Plot in order

1. **A magical piece of wood.** A poor woodcarver named **Geppetto** finds a strange piece of wood. He decides to carve a puppet for company.
2. **The puppet comes alive.** As soon as Geppetto finishes, the puppet **walks and talks by himself**. Geppetto names him **Pinocchio**.
3. **Off to school.** Geppetto, who has very little money, **sells his only coat** to buy Pinocchio a school book. He sends Pinocchio off to school the very next morning.
4. **Tempted by the puppet show.** On the way to school Pinocchio is distracted by a **puppet theatre**. He sells his school book to buy a ticket.
5. **Five gold coins.** The puppeteer at the theatre takes pity on Pinocchio and gives him **five gold coins** to take home to Geppetto.
6. **The Fox and the Cat.** On the road home Pinocchio meets a sly **Fox** pretending to be lame and a **Cat** pretending to be blind. They convince him to come to the **Field of Miracles**, where they say his five gold coins will grow into a tree of gold if he buries them.
7. **Tricked and hanged.** The Fox and the Cat steal his coins and leave him hanging from a tree. He is rescued by the **Blue Fairy / Fairy with Turquoise Hair**.
8. **The lying nose.** The Fairy asks Pinocchio what happened to his coins. He lies — and his **wooden nose grows** longer with each lie. He cries and is given another chance.
9. **A second chance.** The Fairy sends Pinocchio home to Geppetto. But once again, he wanders off and meets the Fox and Cat — and once again he is tricked.
10. **Lazyboy and Toyland.** Pinocchio meets a school friend, "Candlewick" (sometimes called Lampwick), who tells him there is a wonderful land where children do nothing but play and never have lessons — **Toyland**. A coachman drives them there in a wagon pulled by donkeys.
11. **Turned into a donkey.** After weeks of nothing but fun, the magic of Toyland turns Pinocchio and Candlewick into **donkeys**. Pinocchio is sold to a circus.
12. **Into the sea.** A series of accidents lands Pinocchio (now back in puppet form) in the sea, where he is swallowed by an **enormous fish** — a **Terrible Shark** in the original story (a whale in many retellings).
13. **Finding Geppetto.** Inside the belly of the great fish, Pinocchio is amazed to find his father **Geppetto**, who was swallowed too while searching for him. They are reunited.
14. **The escape.** While the giant fish is sleeping with its mouth open, Pinocchio carries Geppetto out and swims them both to safety.
15. **Hard work at last.** Pinocchio finally settles down to take care of his father, working hard each day for food and money, going to school in the evenings, and being honest and good.
16. **A real boy.** As a reward for his kindness and hard work, the **Blue Fairy** appears to Pinocchio one night and turns him into a **real boy**. Geppetto and Pinocchio live happily together.

## Key objects and props

- **The magical piece of wood** Geppetto carves Pinocchio from.
- **Geppetto's coat**, sold to buy Pinocchio's school book.
- **The school book** Pinocchio sells for a puppet-show ticket.
- **The five gold coins** the puppeteer gives him.
- **The "Field of Miracles"** where the Fox and Cat trick him.
- **The lying nose** — grows longer with each lie.
- **The donkey ears, tail, and fur** Pinocchio grows in Toyland.
- **The giant fish / shark** that swallows him and Geppetto.

## Themes and ideas

- **Honesty.** Lying makes Pinocchio's nose grow. Telling the truth makes him human.
- **Hard work versus laziness.** Toyland looks like paradise but turns children into donkeys.
- **Listening to good advice.** The talking cricket warns Pinocchio many times; ignoring him leads to disaster.
- **Loving your father.** Pinocchio's rescue of Geppetto is what finally earns him his reward.
- **Becoming "real."** The whole book is about growing from a wilful puppet into a thoughtful, caring child.

## Tone and style

A short Usborne retelling of a long, episodic Italian novel. Each chapter is a separate adventure with its own danger and its own lesson. Bright illustrations show the puppet with his pointed cap, his long nose growing when he lies, and the enormous fish whose mouth opens like a cave.
