# Little Bear

**Author:** Else Holmelund Minarik
**Illustrator:** Maurice Sendak
**First published:** 1957
**Grade band:** Pre-K / Earliest beginning reader
**Format:** *I Can Read* easy reader (four short stories; first book in the Little Bear series)

---

## Premise

A gentle book of four short stories about a young bear cub called **Little Bear** and his patient, loving **Mother Bear**. In the first story Little Bear keeps asking his mother for warmer clothes to play in the snow. In the second he believes his mother has forgotten his birthday and tries to make his own birthday soup. In the third he pretends to fly to the moon. In the fourth he is in bed at night listing all the things he wishes for. The four stories together show a small boy-bear and his mother in a sweet, slow rhythm of everyday family life.

## Setting

A cosy bear family's house in the woods, the snowy outdoors around the house, and (in Little Bear's imagination) the **moon**. Time covers winter (snow) and a warmer day with friends.

## Characters

- **Little Bear** — the small cub at the centre of every story. Curious, talkative, imaginative, and a little dramatic.
- **Mother Bear** — Little Bear's mother. Patient, warm, and gently amused by everything Little Bear does. She is always there at the end of each story.
- **Father Bear** — appears briefly in some stories.
- **Hen, Duck, and Cat** — Little Bear's animal friends, who come to his birthday in Story 2.

## The four stories

### 1. What Will Little Bear Wear?

- It is very cold outside and Little Bear is shivering in the snow.
- He keeps coming back inside to ask **Mother Bear** for warmer clothes:
  - First a **hat**.
  - Then a **coat**.
  - Then **snow pants**.
- Even after all the clothes, Little Bear is still cold.
- Mother Bear gives him one last thing: she **takes off the hat, coat and snow pants** and points out that Little Bear already has a **fur coat** — his own.
- Little Bear runs out into the snow with **just his fur**, and is warm and happy.

### 2. Birthday Soup

- It is **Little Bear's birthday**, but he cannot find Mother Bear anywhere.
- Little Bear is worried she has **forgotten his birthday**.
- His friends will arrive soon and there is **no cake**.
- He decides to make himself **"Birthday Soup"** for the party instead. He fills a pot with **water, carrots, potatoes, peas, and tomatoes** and puts it on the stove.
- His friends arrive — **Hen**, **Duck**, and **Cat** — and sit down at the table.
- Just as Little Bear is serving up the birthday soup, **Mother Bear arrives** carrying a great **birthday cake**.
- She tells him she could never forget his birthday. Little Bear has his cake **and** his soup, and the friends all have a happy party.

### 3. Little Bear Goes to the Moon

- Little Bear decides he is going to fly to the **moon**.
- He puts on a **space helmet** (a paper bag/hat on his head) and tells Mother Bear his plan.
- Mother Bear gently agrees and tells him to **be back in time for lunch**.
- Little Bear climbs a small **hill** (or tree) and **jumps**. He pretends he has flown all the way to the moon.
- Once "on the moon" he meets a bear who looks just like Mother Bear and lives in a house just like his — because, of course, he is back home.
- He pretends his moon adventure is finished and comes inside for lunch, exactly as Mother Bear had asked.

### 4. Little Bear's Wish

- Little Bear is in bed and tells Mother Bear about all the wonderful things he **wishes for**:
  - to **sit on a cloud**,
  - to find a **Viking boat**,
  - to be a **fast train**,
  - and other impossible wonderful things.
- For each wish, Mother Bear gently says **"You cannot have that wish, my Little Bear"** — but she does not say it to hurt him.
- After many wishes, Mother Bear asks him to make a wish she **can** give him.
- Little Bear wishes for her to tell him **a story** — and she does. He falls asleep listening to it.

## Key objects and props

- **Little Bear's fur coat** — the joke of Story 1: he already has the warmest clothes there are.
- **The hat, coat, and snow pants** Mother Bear puts on him.
- **The big pot of Birthday Soup** with **carrots, potatoes, peas, and tomatoes**.
- **Mother Bear's birthday cake** in Story 2.
- **The space helmet / paper-bag hat** in Story 3.
- **The bed and the story** at the end of Story 4.

## Themes and ideas

- **A patient, loving mother.** Mother Bear answers every silly request with kindness.
- **Imagination.** Little Bear flies to the moon, pretends his back garden is space, and wishes to ride trains and clouds.
- **Family rhythms.** Each story ends with mother and child together — back at home for lunch, having cake, falling asleep to a story.
- **You already have what you need.** Story 1 (he has his own fur coat) and Story 4 (the wish she can give him is just to tell him a story) make the same quiet point.
- **Birthdays and friends.** The middle story is also about friends gathering and a small celebration.

## Tone and style

A very gentle, very simple early reader. Sentences are short and the same words repeat. Maurice Sendak's pen-and-ink illustrations are warm and quiet, with the bears wearing little waistcoats and aprons. The book launched the **I Can Read** series.
