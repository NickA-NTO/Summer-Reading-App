# Biscuit

**Author:** Alyssa Satin Capucilli
**Illustrator:** Pat Schories
**First published:** 1996
**Grade band:** Pre-K / Earliest beginning reader
**Format:** *My First I Can Read* picture book (first book in the Biscuit series)

---

## Premise

A short, gentle, repetitive bedtime story about a **little yellow puppy** called **Biscuit** who simply will not go to sleep. A little girl tries to put him to bed, but Biscuit keeps wanting **one more** thing: a snack, a drink of water, a story, a hug, a kiss, and to be tucked in. Each time the little girl gives him what he wants, Biscuit asks for **one more**. Finally, after all the "one more"s have been used up, Biscuit settles down and falls asleep.

## Setting

The little girl's home — mostly her bedroom and Biscuit's basket. Soft, simple illustrations of a little girl in her pyjamas and a small yellow puppy.

## Characters

- **Biscuit** — a **small yellow puppy**. The whole book is about him. He says **"Woof, woof"** instead of words.
- **The little girl** — Biscuit's owner. She is the one putting him to bed. She is patient and loving.

No other characters are named.

## Plot in order

1. **Bedtime.** It is time for Biscuit to go to bed. The little girl tells him so.
2. **One more snack.** Biscuit doesn't want to go to bed. He says **"Woof, woof"** — he wants **one more snack**. The little girl gives him one.
3. **One more drink.** Biscuit still doesn't want to sleep. He wants **one more drink** of water. The little girl gives him one.
4. **One more story.** Now he wants to hear **one more story**. The little girl reads him one.
5. **One more hug.** He still doesn't want to settle. He wants **one more hug**. The little girl gives him one.
6. **One more kiss.** Then he wants **one more kiss**. The little girl gives him one.
7. **One more light on.** He wants the **light** left on.
8. **One more song.** He wants to hear a song or be sung to.
9. **One more tuck-in.** He wants to be **tucked in** properly.
10. **Sleep at last.** After all of his "**one more**" requests have been answered, Biscuit finally curls up and **goes to sleep** with the little girl.

## Key objects and props

- **Biscuit's bowl** (for the snack).
- **A drink of water**.
- **A bedtime story / book**.
- **Biscuit's basket and blanket** where he sleeps.
- **The little girl's bedroom and night light**.

## Themes and ideas

- **Stalling at bedtime.** A pattern every parent and child knows: just one more thing, please, just one more thing.
- **Routine.** Each request — snack, water, story, hug, kiss — is a familiar bedtime step.
- **Patience.** The little girl never gets cross. She gives Biscuit everything he asks for.
- **Love.** All of Biscuit's stalling is really about wanting more time with his person.
- **Quiet endings.** The book ends with the puppy asleep and the room calm.

## Tone and style

Extremely simple sentences (often just three or four words). The same shape repeats — "Woof, woof. Biscuit wants one more…" — so a child learning to read can predict each page. Soft watercolour illustrations of a pyjama-clad girl and her puppy. The book is designed to be read at bedtime.
