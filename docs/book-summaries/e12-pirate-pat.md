# Pirate Pat

**Author:** Mairi Mackinnon
**Illustrator:** Mike Gordon (for Usborne)
**Publisher:** Usborne (Very First Reading series, Book 1)
**Grade band:** Pre-K / Earliest beginning reader
**Format:** Very short shared-reading phonics book

---

## Premise

A very short shared-reading book for children just learning to read. On a **cold, dark night**, a **pirate** with a tattered coat and a big black pirate hat — **Pirate Pat** — turns up at a small family's seaside cottage. He carries a **treasure map** and says the treasure is buried **right under their house**. **Gran** is not sure what to make of him and tells him to go away, but Pirate Pat digs anyway — and finds the treasure. He shares it with the family. The book is built from just the very first phonics sounds (s, a, t, p, i, n, m, d), so the words are tiny and a beginning reader can sound them out.

## Setting

A small **seaside cottage**, with a path, a garden, and ground that turns out to have **buried treasure** under it.

## Characters

- **Pirate Pat** — the visitor. He wears a tattered coat, a big black pirate hat, and carries a **treasure map**.
- **Gran** — the grandmother of the house. The one who answers the door and objects to the digging.
- **The rest of the family** in the cottage who share the treasure at the end.

## Plot in order

1. **A cold, dark night.** A small family is inside their seaside cottage on a **cold, dark night** when a stranger comes to the door.
2. **A pirate at the door.** It is **Pirate Pat**, wearing a **tattered coat**, a great **black pirate hat**, and carrying a **treasure map**.
3. **A treasure hunt.** Pirate Pat tells them his treasure map shows the treasure buried **right under their house**.
4. **Gran says no.** **Gran** is sceptical and tries to send Pirate Pat away.
5. **Pat digs anyway.** Pirate Pat starts to **dig** in spite of Gran's objections.
6. **The treasure!** To everyone's surprise, Pirate Pat actually **finds the buried treasure**.
7. **A happy sharing.** Pirate Pat **shares the treasure** with the family. Everybody is delighted.

## Key objects and props

- **Pirate Pat's hat** — a big black pirate hat.
- **Pirate Pat's tattered coat**.
- **Pirate Pat's treasure map** — what brings him to the house.
- **The cottage** — a seaside home on a cold, dark night.
- **The pirate's spade** — used to dig in the garden.
- **The buried treasure** — found under the ground next to the house.

## Themes and ideas

- **First-words reading.** This book is part of a phonics scheme that uses only the very first sounds children learn (s, a, t, p, i, n, m, d). Almost every word on the child's pages can be sounded out.
- **Surprises.** A stranger at the door turns out to bring a happy surprise.
- **Sharing.** Pirate Pat doesn't keep the treasure to himself.
- **Don't always judge a visitor.** The grandmother is suspicious — but the pirate is friendly.
- **Adventure right at home.** The treasure isn't on a distant island — it's in their own garden.

## Tone and style

A very short two-voice book. The **adult reads the left-hand page**; the **child reads the right-hand page**, where the words use only the simplest phonic sounds. There are pictures on every spread and short puzzles at the back. The book is designed to be read in one short sitting.
