# Mercy Watson to the Rescue

**Author:** Kate DiCamillo
**Illustrator:** Chris Van Dusen
**First published:** 2005
**Grade band:** Grade 2 / Ages 6–8
**Format:** Short illustrated early chapter book (first in the Mercy Watson series)

---

## Premise

The first book in the Mercy Watson series. **Mercy Watson** is a **pig** — a "porcine wonder" — who lives with **Mr. and Mrs. Watson** and is loved like a daughter. Mercy loves **buttered toast** above all things. One night, scared in her own bed, she creeps into Mr. and Mrs. Watson's room and squeezes onto their bed — which promptly starts to **fall through the floor**. While the Watsons panic, Mercy slips out to look (mostly) for toast, but in the process wakes up the neighbours and indirectly summons the **fire department**, who arrive and save the day. The book ends with everyone — Watsons, firefighters, and the elderly neighbour sisters — sitting in the kitchen eating buttered toast.

## Setting

A small ordinary town. Almost all of the action happens in the **Watsons' house** — Mr. and Mrs. Watson's bedroom, the stairs, the kitchen — and the **house next door**, where two elderly sisters live. There is also a fire engine in the street.

## Characters

- **Mercy Watson** — a pig. The Watsons treat her exactly like their child. She is plump, good-natured, and obsessed with **buttered toast**. She is referred to as a "porcine wonder."
- **Mr. Watson** — Mercy's owner. Loves her dearly. Reads in bed.
- **Mrs. Watson** — Mercy's other owner. Equally loving. Always serves Mercy toast.
- **Eugenia Lincoln** — the cranky elderly sister next door. Believes very firmly that **pigs belong on farms**, not in people's beds. The "no nonsense" sister.
- **Baby Lincoln** — Eugenia's softer, kinder sister. She does not agree that Mercy belongs on a farm and tends to be sweet to her.
- **The firefighters** — called out to rescue the Watsons from the bed crisis.

## Plot in order

1. **Bedtime at the Watsons'.** Mr. and Mrs. Watson tuck Mercy Watson the pig into her own bed. They tell her good night.
2. **Mercy gets scared.** Mercy gets a little scared in her own bed and decides to do what she usually does — climb into bed with Mr. and Mrs. Watson.
3. **A heavy pig.** Mercy is large. When she squeezes onto the Watsons' bed with both of them already in it, the bed **creaks and groans**.
4. **BOOM! CRACK!** With a great noise, the bed gives way and starts to **fall through the bedroom floor** towards the room below. The Watsons cry out for help.
5. **Mercy goes for help.** Mr. Watson asks Mercy to **go and get help**. Mercy gallantly leaps off the falling bed. The Watsons believe she has gone to **alert the fire department**.
6. **What Mercy is actually thinking about.** As soon as Mercy is downstairs, what is really on her mind is **hot toast with a great deal of butter on it**. She wanders off in search of toast.
7. **Across to the neighbours.** Mercy ends up over at the **Lincoln sisters'** house next door.
8. **Eugenia loses her temper.** **Eugenia Lincoln** is furious — pigs belong on farms, not in her house. She chases Mercy, shouting.
9. **Baby tries to help.** **Baby Lincoln** is softer with the pig and tries to calm her sister down.
10. **The phone call.** Through all the noise and panic, somebody finally **telephones the fire department**.
11. **The fire engine arrives.** The **firefighters** come, with sirens. They climb up into the Watsons' bedroom, lift Mr. and Mrs. Watson out of the half-collapsed bed, and bring them safely down.
12. **The hero of the hour.** Although Mercy was really only looking for toast, the Watsons declare that she **went to fetch help**, and so she has rescued them. She is hailed as the hero.
13. **Everyone in the kitchen.** Mr. and Mrs. Watson, the **firefighters**, and **Eugenia and Baby Lincoln** all crowd into the Watson kitchen.
14. **A great deal of buttered toast.** Mrs. Watson makes a huge plate of **hot toast with a great deal of butter on it**, and everyone — even Eugenia — eats together. Mercy is in heaven.

## Key objects and props

- **Mercy's bed**, where she starts the night.
- **Mr. and Mrs. Watson's double bed**, which collapses through the floor.
- **The bedroom floor** that gives way.
- **The Lincolns' house** next door.
- **Eugenia's broom / fury** as she chases the pig.
- **The fire engine and the firefighters.**
- **The kitchen** at the end.
- **The buttered toast** — the most important object in Mercy's whole world.

## Themes and ideas

- **A pig who thinks she is family.** The Watsons treat Mercy as their child; Mercy returns the favour by being part of the household drama.
- **Selfishness and accidental heroism.** Mercy is really chasing toast, but in the process she saves the day. The book is gently funny about this.
- **Neighbours.** Cranky Eugenia and gentle Baby Lincoln are everything a pair of next-door sisters might be — and even cranky Eugenia ends up at the table at the end.
- **Food as comfort.** Toast is mentioned over and over. Everything comes back to toast.
- **Calm in chaos.** The book treats a collapsing bed and a fire engine as a kind of jolly emergency, never frightening.

## Tone and style

A short, very funny early chapter book with bright cartoon-style illustrations on almost every spread. Chapters are very short, often only a page or two. Sentences are simple. The humour comes from how solemnly Mercy treats her search for toast, and how the Watsons treat Mercy completely as a member of the family.
