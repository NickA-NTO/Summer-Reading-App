# Goose on the Loose

**Author:** Phil Roxbee Cox
**Illustrator:** Stephen Cartwright
**Publisher:** Usborne (Phonics Readers series)
**Grade band:** Pre-K / Earliest beginning reader
**Format:** Very short rhyming phonics reader

---

## Premise

A very short, very fast rhyming phonics book about a **rude goose** on a **scooter** who tears through the neighbourhood causing chaos. She **runs other animals off the road**, makes a big noise, and finally **tumbles off her scooter into a cold pool** where some penguins live. The story is built around the **"oo" sound** (goose, loose, moose, pool, scoot, zoom) so children just learning to read can spot the same sound on every page.

## Setting

A simple cartoon village street and the **animals' paths** through it, ending at a **pool / pond**. The illustrations show grassy verges, fences, and tidy backgrounds with the various animals in their own little corners.

## Characters

- **The goose** — the main character. She rides a **scooter** very fast and very rudely. She does not stop or apologise.
- **Other animals on the road** — including (depending on the page) a **moose**, **chickens**, and other small farmyard creatures whom the goose nearly knocks over.
- **Penguins** — appear at the end, in the cold pool where the goose finally lands.

## Plot in order

1. **A goose on the loose.** A goose comes flying down the road on her **scooter**.
2. **Out of her way!** She does not slow down. She makes everyone else **jump off the road** to avoid being hit.
3. **A loud, rude noise.** She makes a great **hullabaloo** as she goes — honking and rushing past.
4. **Chickens and a moose.** Various animals — including a **moose** — have to scatter out of the goose's way as she races past.
5. **A pool ahead.** The goose is going too fast to stop. She zooms straight towards a **pool of cold water** at the end of the road.
6. **In she goes.** The goose **flies off her scooter** and lands with a great splash in the **ice-cold pool**, which turns out to be home to some surprised **penguins**.
7. **A lesson learned.** Soaked, embarrassed and shivering with the penguins, the goose has finally been **stopped** — and has learned her lesson about racing about and not minding other people on the road.

## Key objects and props

- **The goose's scooter** — the centre of the whole story.
- **The road** through the neighbourhood.
- **The "oo" sound** — repeated in goose, loose, moose, pool, zoom, scoot.
- **The cold pool** at the end of the road, full of penguins.

## Themes and ideas

- **The "oo" sound.** This is a phonics reader. The story exists to give children lots of practice spotting and reading "oo" words.
- **Manners on the road.** The goose is rude and reckless. The ending serves her right.
- **What goes too fast tends to crash.** A gentle moral built into a funny rhyme.
- **A surprise ending.** The cold pool of penguins is unexpected and silly.

## Tone and style

A tiny book. Almost every page is a couple of lines of **rhyming verse**, with one cartoon picture and a few simple "oo" words for the reader to recognise. The pictures are bright and cartoony, with one big silly moment per spread. The story can be read in under a minute.

- The big idea of the book: do not race about rudely — you may end up in a very cold pool.
