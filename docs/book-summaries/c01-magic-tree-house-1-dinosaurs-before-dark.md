# Magic Tree House #1: Dinosaurs Before Dark

**Author:** Mary Pope Osborne
**Illustrator:** Sal Murdocca
**First published:** 1992
**Grade band:** Grade 3 / Ages 6–9
**Format:** Early chapter book (first book in the Magic Tree House series)

---

## Premise

Two siblings — eight-year-old **Jack** and his seven-year-old sister **Annie** — find a magical **tree house** in the woods near their home in **Frog Creek, Pennsylvania**. The tree house is filled with books. When Jack wishes on a picture of a dinosaur, the tree house spins and carries the children back **65 million years** to the **Cretaceous period**, the age of the dinosaurs. They meet a friendly **Pteranodon**, a calm **Triceratops**, **Anatosauruses** with their babies, and are chased by a **Tyrannosaurus Rex**. Jack finds a mysterious **gold medallion** with the letter **M** on it. The children wish their way home before dark, with the medallion still in Jack's pocket — proof that the adventure was real.

## Setting

The whole book has two settings:

- **Frog Creek, Pennsylvania** — present day, in the woods near Jack and Annie's house. This is where the **magic tree house** is found.
- **The Cretaceous period, 65 million years ago** — a prehistoric world with rivers, magnolia trees, mud nests, and dinosaurs. Once the tree house spins, it lands in a giant **oak tree** in this prehistoric forest.

## Characters

- **Jack** — eight years old. The older sibling. Sensible, cautious, wears **glasses**, carries a **backpack with a notebook and pencil** in which he writes down facts about every creature he sees.
- **Annie** — seven years old. Jack's younger sister. Brave, impulsive, and the first to leap into adventure. She is unafraid of the dinosaurs and gives them names.
- **Their parents** — mentioned at home in Frog Creek. Not present in the adventure.
- **A friendly Pteranodon** — a large flying reptile with leathery wings, gentle and helpful, who lets Jack ride on his back.
- **Triceratops** — a huge three-horned dinosaur the children watch from a distance. Calm, eats flowers off a magnolia tree.
- **Anatosaurus (duck-billed dinosaur)** — protective parents with babies in a **mud nest**.
- **Tyrannosaurus Rex** — the giant predator, far bigger than a truck, who chases Jack at the climax.
- **The mysterious "M"** — whoever built the tree house and filled it with the magic books. Not seen in this book; only the **gold medallion** with the letter M is left behind.

## Plot in order

1. **A walk in the woods.** One afternoon in **Frog Creek, Pennsylvania**, Jack and Annie are walking in the woods near their house.
2. **The tallest tree house.** Annie spots an enormous **tree house** high up in an oak tree. There is a **rope ladder** hanging down.
3. **Going up.** Annie climbs up first; Jack follows. The tree house is full of **books** — stacks and stacks of them.
4. **A wish.** Jack picks up a book about dinosaurs. He points at a picture of a **Pteranodon** and says he wishes he could see a real one.
5. **The tree house spins.** The wind starts to blow. The tree house **spins faster and faster** until everything goes still.
6. **Where they are.** When they open their eyes and look out the window, they are in a different forest. A **Pteranodon** flies past. They are in the **Cretaceous period**, 65 million years ago.
7. **Jack and the Pteranodon.** They climb down. The Pteranodon is friendly. Jack writes facts in his **notebook**: leathery wings, lives in the time of the dinosaurs.
8. **Triceratops.** They climb a hilltop and look across to see a huge **Triceratops** — a three-horned dinosaur "bigger than a truck" — calmly eating flowers off a **magnolia tree**. Jack writes in his notebook: **"Eats flowers."**
9. **The duck-billed dinosaurs.** They find a **mud nest** of baby **Anatosauruses** (duck-billed dinosaurs). The parent dinosaurs come and bellow at the children. Jack and Annie freeze and stare at the ground; the big dinosaurs calm down.
10. **The gold medallion.** As Jack moves, his foot kicks something gleaming in the grass — a small **gold medallion** with the letter **M** stamped on it. He picks it up.
11. **The T-Rex.** Suddenly a **Tyrannosaurus Rex** comes crashing through the trees. The Anatosauruses run. The T-Rex turns towards Jack and Annie.
12. **Running for the tree.** The children sprint back towards their oak tree. The T-Rex chases.
13. **Annie reaches safety.** Annie gets to the tree house first and starts climbing the rope ladder.
14. **The Pteranodon saves Jack.** Jack is not going to make it. The friendly **Pteranodon swoops down**, lets Jack scramble onto his back, and **flies him to safety** in the tree house.
15. **Jack thanks Annie.** Back in the tree house, Jack thanks Annie — really, the Pteranodon — for saving his life.
16. **Wishing home.** Jack quickly finds the book about **Frog Creek, Pennsylvania**, points at a picture of their woods, and says he wishes they could go home. The tree house spins again.
17. **Home before dark.** When they open their eyes, they are back in their own woods. The sun has barely moved — **no time has passed** at home. They are back in Frog Creek **before dark**, as their parents had told them.
18. **The proof.** Jack reaches into his pocket and pulls out the **gold medallion** with the **letter M** on it. The adventure was real.
19. **A new mystery.** They wonder who built the tree house and who **M** is. They make a quiet promise to come back the next day.

## Key objects and props

- **The tree house** in the tall oak — full of books, with a rope ladder.
- **Jack's backpack, notebook, and pencil** — he writes down every dinosaur fact.
- **The dinosaur book** — the one Jack opens when he makes the wish.
- **The picture of the Pteranodon** — the page Jack touches to start the adventure.
- **The gold medallion** stamped with the letter **M** — the only thing Jack brings back.
- **The book of Pennsylvania / Frog Creek** — the one Jack uses to wish them home.

## Themes and ideas

- **Curiosity and learning.** Jack reads, takes notes, and learns about dinosaurs in real time.
- **Bravery in two flavours.** Annie is fearless and goes first. Jack is careful but goes anyway. Together they make a good team.
- **Magic in ordinary places.** The tree house is just in the woods near their house.
- **Adventure has rules.** They must wish to come home. They must get back before dark.
- **Mystery.** The medallion and the letter M leave the reader (and the children) wanting to know more.

## Tone and style

Very short chapters with line-drawing illustrations. Simple, clear sentences. Each chapter ends on a small cliffhanger, which keeps newly fluent readers turning pages. The book balances **dinosaur facts** (Jack's notebook entries) with an exciting magical plot.
