# Jack and the Beanstalk

**Author:** Traditional English folktale, retold for Usborne
**Earliest known printed version:** *The Story of Jack Spriggins and the Enchanted Bean* (1734); Benjamin Tabart's *The History of Jack and the Bean-Stalk* (1807)
**Grade band:** Grade 1 / Ages 5–7
**Format:** Illustrated retelling for early readers (Usborne)

---

## Premise

A poor boy named **Jack** lives with his widowed **mother** in a cottage. They have no money left, so the mother sends Jack to **market** to sell their only **cow**. On the way Jack meets a stranger who trades him a handful of **magic beans** for the cow. Jack's mother is furious and throws the beans out of the window. By morning, an enormous **beanstalk** has grown from the ground up into the clouds. Jack climbs it and finds a **giant's castle** in the sky. Over a few trips up the beanstalk he steals a **bag of gold**, a **hen that lays golden eggs**, and a **golden harp that sings**. On the final trip the harp shouts for the giant. Jack races down the beanstalk and **chops it down with an axe**. The giant falls and is killed, and Jack and his mother live happily ever after.

## Setting

Three places that connect in one beanstalk:

- **Jack's poor cottage and garden** down on the ground, with his mother and the cow.
- **The road to market** where he meets the stranger.
- **The land at the top of the beanstalk**, including the **giant's castle** in the clouds.

## Characters

- **Jack** — a young boy. Daring, curious, and a little cheeky.
- **Jack's mother** — a poor widow. Worries about Jack. Furious when he trades the cow for beans.
- **The cow** — the family's only animal. Sold at the start of the story.
- **The mysterious stranger / old man** — appears once on the road. Offers the magic beans.
- **The Giant** — an enormous, fearsome man living in the castle in the clouds. Says **"Fee-fi-fo-fum!"** when he smells a human.
- **The Giant's wife** — friendlier than her husband. Hides Jack from him on his first visit.
- **The hen that lays golden eggs.**
- **The talking golden harp** that plays and sings by itself.

## Plot in order

1. **Poor and hungry.** Jack and his mother are poor and have no money. The only thing of value they own is their **cow**.
2. **Off to market.** Jack's mother tells him to take the cow to market and sell her for the best price he can.
3. **The magic beans.** On the way, Jack meets a strange old man who offers to swap the cow for a handful of **magic beans**. The old man promises the beans will bring great fortune. Jack takes the deal and walks home with the beans.
4. **Mother is angry.** Jack's mother is furious when she sees the beans. She **throws them out of the window** and sends Jack to bed without any supper.
5. **The beanstalk.** During the night, the beans grow into an enormous **beanstalk** that reaches up into the clouds.
6. **Jack climbs.** In the morning, Jack climbs the beanstalk all the way up to a strange land above the clouds.
7. **The castle in the sky.** At the top, Jack finds a huge **castle**. He knocks on the door. The **giant's wife** answers and warns him her husband eats little boys, but takes pity on Jack and hides him.
8. **"Fee-fi-fo-fum!"** The giant arrives, sniffing the air: **"Fee-fi-fo-fum! I smell the blood of an Englishman!"** His wife tells him there is no one there. The giant eats his enormous dinner, counts his **bags of gold coins**, and falls asleep.
9. **The bag of gold.** When the giant is snoring, Jack creeps out, grabs **a bag of gold**, and climbs back down the beanstalk to his mother. They are happy for a while.
10. **Up again.** When the gold runs out, Jack climbs the beanstalk **again**. He hides at the castle a second time.
11. **The hen that lays golden eggs.** This time, when the giant falls asleep, Jack sees the giant calling to a **hen**. The giant says **"Lay!"** and the hen lays a beautiful **golden egg**. Jack steals the hen and climbs back down. Now whenever they want money, the hen lays a golden egg.
12. **Up a third time.** Jack cannot resist going up **once more**. This time he is after the **golden harp** that plays sweet music all by itself.
13. **The harp shouts.** Jack creeps out and grabs the harp. But the harp is enchanted and **calls out**, "**Master! Master!**" The giant **wakes up** and starts to chase Jack.
14. **Down the beanstalk.** Jack races down the beanstalk with the harp under his arm, the giant climbing furiously after him.
15. **The axe.** As soon as Jack's feet touch the ground, he shouts to his mother for an **axe**. He **chops the beanstalk down**.
16. **The giant falls.** With a great crash, the beanstalk and the giant come tumbling down. The **giant is killed**.
17. **Happy ever after.** Jack and his mother now have a bag of gold, a hen that lays golden eggs, and a golden harp that plays by itself. They are no longer poor and live happily ever after.

## Key objects and props

- **The cow** — the family's only animal.
- **The handful of magic beans** — what Jack trades the cow for.
- **The enormous beanstalk** that grows overnight.
- **The giant's castle in the clouds.**
- **The bag of gold coins** — Jack's first treasure.
- **The hen that lays golden eggs** — Jack's second treasure.
- **The golden harp that plays itself** — Jack's third treasure.
- **The axe** Jack uses to cut down the beanstalk.

## Themes and ideas

- **Bravery and risk.** Jack climbs a magical plant into a giant's castle. Three times.
- **Cleverness versus brute strength.** Jack is small, the giant is huge — but Jack escapes again and again.
- **Trickery and theft.** Jack steals from the giant. Different retellings have different views on whether this makes Jack a hero or a thief.
- **Believing in magic.** Jack's mother throws the beans away — but Jack was right; they really were magic.
- **From rags to riches.** A poor boy with nothing ends the story with three magical treasures.

## Tone and style

A short, exciting fairy tale retelling for young children. The famous **"Fee-fi-fo-fum!"** chant and the **giant's enormous footsteps** are the dramatic centre of the book. Bright illustrations show a tall green beanstalk, a boy in a cap, a giant's table, and a tiny figure climbing down with a harp under his arm.
