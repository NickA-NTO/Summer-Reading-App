# Stellaluna

**Author and Illustrator:** Janell Cannon
**First published:** 1993
**Grade band:** Grade 2 / Ages 5–8
**Format:** Illustrated picture book

---

## Premise

A baby **fruit bat** named **Stellaluna** is knocked out of her mother's arms by an owl and falls into a nest of baby birds. Their mother bird agrees to raise her — but only if she lives like a bird: eat bugs, sleep at night, and never hang upside down. Stellaluna tries hard, but it doesn't suit her. Eventually she meets other bats, learns who she really is, finds her mother again, and brings her bird siblings to meet her real family. They all agree that even though bats and birds are very different, they can still be friends.

## Setting

A warm forest — the jungles of **Africa**. Most of the action happens in and around a single tree: the nest where Stellaluna grows up with the birds, and the night sky where bats fly. There is no town or human setting.

## Characters

- **Stellaluna** — the main character. A young **fruit bat** (drawn as a **Gambian epauletted fruit bat**) with soft fur and big eyes. Loving, polite, and a little anxious about doing things wrong.
- **Mother Bat** — Stellaluna's real mother, a fruit bat. Lost in the first scene; reunited at the end.
- **An owl** — the predator who attacks them at the start. Not given a name.
- **Mother Bird** — a small mother bird who finds Stellaluna in her nest and agrees to raise her, on bird-house rules.
- **Pip, Flitter, and Flap** — Mother Bird's three baby birds (small sparrow-like birds). They grow up alongside Stellaluna.
- **Other bats** — the bats Stellaluna finally meets when she is older.

## Plot in order

1. **Mother and baby bat.** In a warm jungle one night, **Mother Bat** is out flying with her tiny baby, **Stellaluna**, clinging to her chest. They are looking for food (ripe, soft fruit).
2. **An owl attacks.** Out of the dark, **an owl** swoops down on them. In the chaos, Stellaluna is **knocked from her mother's arms** and falls down through the trees.
3. **Lost.** Stellaluna catches hold of a small branch and hangs there, exhausted, until she can hold on no longer. She falls again — and this time lands inside a **bird's nest** high in a tree.
4. **The bird family.** In the nest are **three baby birds — Pip, Flitter, and Flap** — and their **mother bird**. They have never seen anything like Stellaluna.
5. **Mother Bird's rules.** Mother Bird agrees that Stellaluna can stay — but she must do everything the bird way. The rules are:
   - **Eat bugs and worms**, not fruit.
   - **Sleep at night** instead of by day.
   - **Do not hang upside down by her feet.**
6. **Trying to be a bird.** Stellaluna does her best. She forces down beetles and worms even though they taste awful. She tries to sleep at night and stay still in the nest. She tries to stand upright instead of hanging.
7. **One slip.** One day Stellaluna **hangs upside down by her feet** anyway, because it's natural to her. Mother Bird is furious and warns her again that bats are different from birds.
8. **Learning to fly.** Stellaluna learns to **fly** along with Pip, Flitter, and Flap. She is bigger and stronger than the baby birds.
9. **The night flight.** The four young ones are out flying one evening when night begins to fall. The birds cannot see in the dark, and they hurry back to the nest, leaving Stellaluna behind.
10. **Alone in the dark.** Stellaluna's wings get tired. She stops to rest on a tree and **hangs by her thumbs**, alone.
11. **A bat appears.** Another bat finds her hanging there and is surprised. It asks why she is hanging by her thumbs instead of her feet. More bats come and crowd around her.
12. **Meeting her own kind.** As Stellaluna tells the bats her story, one of them recognises her — it is her own **Mother Bat**, who had been searching for her. They are reunited.
13. **Discovering she is a bat.** Stellaluna learns that fruit bats **see at night, eat fruit, and hang upside down by their feet**. She tastes a piece of soft fruit. It is much, much better than bugs.
14. **Sharing the news.** Stellaluna flies back to the nest to find Pip, Flitter, and Flap and brings them out to meet the other bats.
15. **Bats vs. birds.** Pip, Flitter, and Flap try hanging upside down with Stellaluna and don't like it. Stellaluna sees the world the bird way and the bat way and realises they are very different.
16. **Friends, but different.** The book ends with the four young friends agreeing that, even though bats and birds are different, they will always be **friends**.

## Key objects and props

- **The night sky** of the jungle — where Stellaluna and her mother are flying when the owl attacks.
- **The owl** — the danger that starts the story.
- **The bird's nest** high in the tree — Stellaluna's home through her childhood.
- **Bugs and fruit** — the two kinds of food that mark the difference between birds and bats.
- **Hanging by the feet vs. standing upright** — the visual joke and the key biological difference.
- **The branch where Stellaluna hangs alone** at night — where the other bats find her.

## Themes and ideas

- **Identity and belonging.** Stellaluna doesn't know she is a bat. The story is about her finding out.
- **Family by choice and family by birth.** Mother Bird raises her. Mother Bat is her real mother. Both kinds of family matter.
- **Being yourself even when you don't fit in.** Stellaluna keeps trying to act like a bird, but she is happier as a bat.
- **Difference is not the same as separation.** The birds and the bat are very different, but the book ends with them as friends.
- **Reunion.** A child lost from her mother is found again — a warm, reassuring ending.

## Tone and style

A short picture book with very detailed, large illustrations of bats, birds, leaves, and night skies. The text is calm and reads aloud well. The book is famous for its richly painted artwork. There are also short factual notes about real bats at the back of the book.
