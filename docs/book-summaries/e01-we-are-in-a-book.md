# We Are in a Book!

**Author and Illustrator:** Mo Willems
**First published:** 2010
**Grade band:** Pre-K / Beginning reader
**Format:** Easy-reader picture book (part of the Elephant & Piggie series)

---

## Premise

**Gerald** the elephant and his best friend **Piggie** suddenly realise something amazing — **a reader is looking at them.** They are **in a book!** They have great fun discovering that they have power over the reader, especially when they make the reader say a silly word ("**banana**"). But then Gerald notices that the book is going to **end** — and he gets very upset. Piggie comes up with a clever plan: they ask the reader to **read the book again**. Crisis solved, and the book ends with Gerald and Piggie inviting the reader to come back.

## Setting

Almost nothing but blank white space. The two friends and their thought-bubbles are all the reader ever sees on the page. The book itself, with its pages and its reader, is the whole setting.

## Characters

- **Gerald** — a grey **elephant**. Nervous, dramatic, careful. He is the worrier of the duo.
- **Piggie** — a pink **pig**. Cheerful, bouncy, optimistic. She often has the bright idea.
- **The reader** — whoever is reading the book. Becomes a character because Gerald and Piggie keep talking directly to them.

## Plot in order

1. **Just sitting around.** The book opens on a calm white page with Gerald and Piggie just hanging out.
2. **Being watched.** One of them — **Piggie** — looks up, sees something, and tells Gerald: a **reader** is looking at them.
3. **Discovery.** Together they realise something astonishing: **they are in a book!** They are very excited.
4. **A power discovered.** They figure out they can make the reader **say a word out loud**. They pick the funniest word they can think of: **"BANANA."**
5. **Making the reader say "banana."** When the reader gets to the word "banana" on the page, the reader has to say it. The two of them laugh and laugh and want to do it again.
6. **A horrible thought.** Then Gerald reads ahead on the page. He realises that books **end**. He becomes very upset: **the book will end on page 57**.
7. **Panic.** Gerald spirals. He doesn't want the book to end. He doesn't want to stop being a character.
8. **Piggie's plan.** Piggie has an idea. She turns to the reader and asks if they would mind **reading the book again** when it ends.
9. **The polite request.** Gerald and Piggie speak directly to the reader and ask, very nicely, if the reader will **please read the book again**.
10. **The end.** The book ends, looping back to the start (which is why, on the very first page, Piggie says **"Hello!"** and **thanks the reader**).

## Key objects and props

- **The page number** Gerald reads ahead to — **page 57**, the last page.
- **The word "banana"** — the word that gives the duo their first thrill of power.
- **The book itself** — the central object. Gerald and Piggie can see its pages.
- **The reader** — also kind of a prop. Gerald and Piggie keep talking directly to them.

## Themes and ideas

- **Books and reading.** This is a book about being a character in a book — a gentle joke for beginning readers.
- **Endings.** Gerald is genuinely upset that the book will end. The book is quietly about loss and how to face it.
- **Asking for what you want.** Piggie's solution is to just **ask the reader nicely** for what they want.
- **Friendship.** Gerald panics; Piggie has the answer. The two need each other.
- **Beginnings inside endings.** The clever last move is to make the ending into a new beginning.

## Tone and style

A very short, very funny Elephant & Piggie book. Very few words on each page, mostly in big speech bubbles. Lots of white space. Gerald's emotions go from cheerful to terrified to relieved in big visual leaps. The whole book is a single conversation between Gerald, Piggie, and the reader.

## Quick fact bank (useful for question writing)

- The two main characters: **Gerald** (the **elephant**) and **Piggie** (the **pig**).
- What Gerald and Piggie figure out at the start: **they are in a book!**
- Who else they realise is "there": a **reader** is looking at them.
- The power they discover they have: making the reader **say a word out loud**.
- The silly word they make the reader say: **"BANANA."**
- What upsets Gerald in the middle of the book: he realises **the book will end**.
- The last page number Gerald reads: **page 57**.
- Whose idea is the solution: **Piggie's**.
- What Piggie asks the reader to do: **read the book again** (please).
- Why Piggie thanks the reader on page 1: because the reader has agreed to **read the book again**.
- The big idea of the book: a book is between **you and the characters in it**.
- The series this book is part of: the **Elephant & Piggie** series.
