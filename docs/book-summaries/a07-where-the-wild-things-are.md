# Where the Wild Things Are

**Author and Illustrator:** Maurice Sendak
**First published:** 1963
**Grade band:** Grade 1 / Ages 4–8
**Format:** Short illustrated picture book

---

## Premise

A small boy named **Max** is sent to bed without his supper for being too wild. From his bedroom, a magical forest grows, an ocean appears, and Max sails away to a far-off island **where the wild things are**. The wild things make him their king, there is a great **wild rumpus**, and then Max sails home — to find his supper still waiting for him in his room, still hot.

## Setting

Two settings that swap into each other:

- **Max's home / bedroom** — where the story begins and ends.
- **An imaginary island "where the wild things are"** — reached by a long sea voyage. The island has its own forest and is home to the large, hairy, horned monsters called the wild things.

The journey is told as if the ocean and the boat appear inside Max's own room and the forest grows out of the bedroom walls.

## Characters

- **Max** — a small boy. The hero of the story. He wears a **wolf suit** with ears and a tail.
- **Max's mother** — heard but never seen in the pictures. She calls Max a "wild thing" and sends him to bed without supper.
- **The wild things** — a group of large, monstrous creatures with horns, claws, and yellow eyes. They are scary at first but become Max's loyal subjects.

The wild things are not given names in the book.

## Plot in order

1. **Max in his wolf suit.** Max is dressed in a **wolf suit** and is making **mischief of one kind and another** around the house: he chases the family dog with a fork, hammers a nail into the wall, and builds a tent with a sheet.
2. **The argument.** Max's mother calls him **"WILD THING!"** Max yells back: **"I'll eat you up!"**
3. **Sent to bed.** Max is sent to bed **without eating anything**.
4. **The forest grows.** That very night, in Max's room, a **forest grows** — trees and vines push up out of the walls and the ceiling.
5. **The ocean.** An **ocean** tumbles by, and a **private boat** appears for Max, with his name on it.
6. **The long voyage.** Max sails off **through night and day**, and **in and out of weeks**, and **almost over a year**, to the place where the wild things are.
7. **Arrival.** When Max arrives, the wild things meet him with terrifying behaviour: they **roar their terrible roars**, **gnash their terrible teeth**, **roll their terrible eyes**, and **show their terrible claws**.
8. **The magic trick.** Max isn't afraid. He tames the wild things by **staring into their yellow eyes without blinking once**.
9. **King of all wild things.** The wild things are frightened of him. They call him **the most wild thing of all** and **make him king**.
10. **"Let the wild rumpus start!"** Max gives the order, and a great **wild rumpus** begins — Max and the wild things dance, swing from trees, parade, and howl together for three picture spreads with no words.
11. **End of the rumpus.** Max says **stop** and sends the wild things off to **bed without their supper**.
12. **Lonely Max.** Up in his royal place, Max begins to feel lonely. He **wants to be where someone loves him best of all**.
13. **The good smell.** From far away, across the world, Max **smells good things to eat**.
14. **Time to leave.** Max gives up being king and gets back into his private boat.
15. **The wild things plead.** The wild things beg him: **"Oh please don't go—we'll eat you up—we love you so!"** Max says **"No!"**
16. **The voyage home.** Max sails back **in and out of weeks** and **through a day** and **into the night of his very own room**.
17. **Supper is waiting.** When he gets back, his **supper is waiting for him** in his room — **and it was still hot.**

## Key objects and props

- **Max's wolf suit** — the suit he wears throughout the story.
- **The fork** he uses to chase the dog.
- **The hammer and nail** in the wall.
- **The forest** that grows in his bedroom.
- **The ocean** that tumbles by.
- **The private boat** with Max's name on it.
- **The wild things' yellow eyes** — what Max stares into to tame them.
- **The crown and king's costume** Max wears once he is made king.
- **Max's supper** at the end — still hot.

## Themes and ideas

- **Anger and imagination.** Max's wild journey takes place between being sent to bed and waking up; it is what his anger and imagination do with the punishment.
- **Big feelings, safe space.** Being a "wild thing" is allowed in the dream world; being safe is allowed in the real one.
- **Power and loneliness.** Being king of the wild things looks fun, but Max gets lonely and wants to be where someone loves him.
- **Forgiveness and home.** The supper waiting for Max is the book's gentle way of saying he is still loved.
- **The "wild rumpus".** A celebration of pure noisy joy — running, howling, climbing — that adults sometimes try to shut down.

## Tone and style

Very few words; the book is famously short. Sentences are long and run on like sea-swells. The illustrations grow larger and larger on the page as Max gets deeper into the wild place, until the words disappear completely for the rumpus. Then the words come back as he sails home. The book combines a scary, dreamlike feel with a deeply comforting ending.
