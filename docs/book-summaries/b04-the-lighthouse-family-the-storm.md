# The Lighthouse Family: The Storm

**Author:** Cynthia Rylant
**Illustrator:** Preston McDaniels
**First published:** 2002
**Grade band:** Grade 2 / Ages 6–9
**Format:** Illustrated early chapter book (first book in the Lighthouse Family series)

---

## Premise

The first book in the **Lighthouse Family** series. **Pandora**, a lonely **cat** who is the keeper of a lighthouse on a small island, lives quietly tending the lamp that keeps sailors safe. After a terrible storm, she finds a half-drowned **dog** named **Seabold** washed up on the rocks. She takes him in and nurses him back to health while he repairs his small boat. The two of them then rescue **three small mice** — **Lila, Whistler, and Tiny** — adrift in a wooden crate on the sea. By the end of the book, the four animals have become a family, and no-one is lonely any more.

## Setting

A small rocky **island** in the middle of the sea, with a tall **lighthouse** on it. The lighthouse is Pandora's home. There is no town and no other person around. Most of the action takes place at the lighthouse, on the beach, and out at sea in stormy weather. The story spans winter into spring.

## Characters

- **Pandora** — a cat. She is the **keeper of the lighthouse**. She is calm, careful, and very lonely at the start of the book. As a young cat she was once saved at sea by a lighthouse, and so she has dedicated her life to keeping a lighthouse for other sailors.
- **Seabold** — a dog. A sailor who travels alone in his **small boat**. He has spent most of his life at sea on his own, and is also used to being alone.
- **Lila** — a young mouse. The oldest of the three mice siblings.
- **Whistler** — another young mouse. Lila's brother.
- **Tiny** — the smallest of the three mice. A baby mouse. She is **ill** when they are rescued.

The mice's parents do not appear in the book; the three mice are alone in their crate on the sea.

## Plot in order

1. **The lighthouse keeper.** **Pandora the cat** lives alone in a tall **lighthouse** on a small island. Every evening she lights the lamp at the top. Every morning she puts it out. She is **lonely** but takes her work very seriously.
2. **Why Pandora is here.** When Pandora was a young cat, she and her father were caught in a terrible storm at sea. They were saved by a lighthouse's light, and from that day she knew she would grow up to be **a lighthouse keeper** herself.
3. **A storm at sea.** A great **storm** rises one night. The sea is wild. Pandora keeps the lamp burning through the night to help any sailors who might be in danger.
4. **A washed-up sailor.** In the morning, after the storm has passed, Pandora finds a **dog** on the rocks at the foot of the lighthouse. His **small boat** has been smashed up in the storm and he himself is hurt and barely conscious.
5. **Bringing him in.** Pandora carries him up to the lighthouse and looks after him. He sleeps for a long time. When he finally wakes, he tells her his name: **Seabold**.
6. **Recovering.** Seabold stays at the lighthouse to heal. The two animals are both shy and used to being alone, but they slowly become friends.
7. **Repairing the boat.** As soon as he is strong enough, Seabold begins to **repair his boat**. He works on it day after day, planning to set sail again when it is fit.
8. **A crate in the sea.** One windy day in **spring**, Pandora and Seabold spot something tiny bobbing on the waves far from the island — a wooden **crate**.
9. **Seabold sets out.** Seabold launches his **partly-repaired boat** and sails out to investigate. When he reaches the crate, he finds **three small mice** huddled inside, soaking wet and weak.
10. **Three orphaned mice.** Seabold brings them back to the lighthouse. The two older mice introduce themselves as **Lila** and **Whistler**. Their baby sister **Tiny** is sick.
11. **Caring for the mice.** Pandora and Seabold care for Lila, Whistler, and especially Tiny, until she is well again. The mice are very grateful.
12. **A change of plan.** Seabold's boat is nearly mended, but with three small mice now living at the lighthouse, no-one wants the family to split apart.
13. **A lighthouse family.** By the end of the book, **Pandora, Seabold, Lila, Whistler, and Tiny** have become a family together in the lighthouse. The cat keeper, the sailor dog, and the three little mice are no longer alone.

## Key objects and props

- **The lighthouse** and its **lamp** at the top — Pandora's lifelong work.
- **Seabold's small boat** — wrecked in the storm, mended through the book.
- **The rocks** at the foot of the lighthouse where Seabold is found.
- **The wooden crate** on the sea — the way the three mice arrive.
- **Pandora's kitchen** in the lighthouse, where she nurses Seabold and then the mice.

## Themes and ideas

- **Loneliness and company.** Both Pandora and Seabold are used to being alone. The book is about how that changes.
- **A family is the people who care for you.** A cat, a dog, and three mice make a perfectly happy household.
- **Helping others.** Lighthouses save sailors. Pandora saves Seabold. They both save the mice.
- **Storms have a purpose.** A storm wrecks Seabold's boat, but it is the same storm that brings him and Pandora together.
- **Kindness to small creatures.** Even tiny mice in trouble are worth a dangerous sail to rescue.

## Tone and style

A short, warm, quietly told chapter book with rich watercolour illustrations on most spreads. The chapters are short and unrushed. The mood is calm even when the events are dramatic (a storm, a rescue). The vocabulary is gentle and just a step up from the very earliest readers.
