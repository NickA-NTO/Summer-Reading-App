# Corduroy

**Author and Illustrator:** Don Freeman
**First published:** 1968
**Grade band:** Grade 1 / Ages 3–7
**Format:** Illustrated picture book

---

## Premise

A small teddy bear named **Corduroy** lives on a shelf in the toy department of a large store, waiting for someone to take him home. A little girl called **Lisa** loves him at first sight, but her mother won't buy him — he's **missing a button** on his shoulder strap. That night Corduroy goes on a small adventure through the store to find his missing button. The next day Lisa comes back with her own piggy-bank money and buys him, sews on a new button at home, and the bear gets the friend he has always wanted.

## Setting

Two main settings:

- **A large department store** — its **toy section**, its **escalator**, and the **furniture department** upstairs (with beds and mattresses and a lamp). Most of the night adventure happens here.
- **Lisa's home** — a small apartment where Lisa takes Corduroy and sews on his button. The book ends here.

## Characters

- **Corduroy** — a small brown teddy bear in **green corduroy overalls**. The strap on his shoulder is **missing a button**.
- **Lisa** — a little girl who falls in love with Corduroy and saves up her own money to buy him.
- **Lisa's mother** — the parent who at first refuses to buy Corduroy because he is shopworn and missing a button.
- **The night watchman / security guard** — the man on duty at the store at night who finds Corduroy after he falls.

No other characters are named.

## Plot in order

1. **In the toy department.** Corduroy lives in the **toy department** of a **big store**, on a shelf with other toys, all waiting to be taken home.
2. **Lisa sees him.** A little girl named **Lisa** comes by and loves Corduroy at once. She tells her mother she has always wanted a teddy bear just like him.
3. **The missing button.** Lisa's **mother** points out that the little bear's **shoulder strap is missing a button** and that they have already spent a lot of money. They leave without him.
4. **Corduroy is sad.** Corduroy realises he has lost a button from his **shoulder strap**, and decides he must find one of his own.
5. **The escalator.** That night, when the store is closed, Corduroy climbs down off his shelf and steps onto an **escalator**, which carries him up to the **furniture department**. He thinks it must be a palace.
6. **The mattress.** Corduroy sees a bed with a big **mattress** in the furniture department. He spots a round **mattress button** on the mattress and thinks it is his lost button. He tugs at it with all his strength.
7. **The fall.** The button will not come off, but Corduroy pulls so hard he topples backwards off the bed. As he falls he **knocks over a lamp**.
8. **The night watchman.** A **night watchman** hears the crash and comes to investigate. He finds the little bear on the floor.
9. **Back to the shelf.** The watchman gently picks Corduroy up, carries him back down the escalator, and sets him back on his shelf in the **toy department**, where he belongs.
10. **Lisa returns.** The next morning Lisa comes back to the store. She tells the salesperson she has saved up money from her **piggy bank** and uses it to buy Corduroy. She introduces herself: **"My name is Lisa."**
11. **Going home.** Lisa carries Corduroy home to her own small bedroom in her family's apartment.
12. **The new button.** Lisa **sews a new button** onto Corduroy's shoulder strap.
13. **Friends.** Corduroy looks at Lisa and tells her she must be a friend — he has **"always wanted a friend."** Lisa hugs him and says **"You must be a friend"** — and she has always wanted a friend like him too. The book ends with the two of them hugging.

## Key objects and props

- **Corduroy's green overalls** — what he wears throughout the book.
- **The missing button** on his shoulder strap — the cause of the whole story.
- **The shelf** in the toy department where Corduroy waits.
- **The escalator** that takes Corduroy up to the furniture department.
- **The mattress and its tufted button** — what Corduroy mistakes for his own button.
- **The lamp** Corduroy knocks over.
- **The night watchman's flashlight** — used to find Corduroy in the dark store.
- **Lisa's piggy bank** — the source of the money to buy Corduroy.
- **The needle and thread** Lisa uses to sew on the new button at home.

## Themes and ideas

- **Wanting a friend.** Corduroy's whole story is about not being chosen. Lisa is the one who chooses him.
- **Love that doesn't need things to be perfect.** Lisa wants Corduroy *even though* he is missing a button.
- **Earning what you want.** Lisa pays for Corduroy with money she has saved herself.
- **Small adventures.** A trip up an escalator in the dark is a huge adventure for a little stuffed bear.
- **Belonging.** Corduroy goes from a shelf, to a strange palace, back to the shelf, and finally to a home of his own.

## Tone and style

A short, warm, gently funny picture book with soft watercolour illustrations and the iconic green-overall bear. The narration is plain and direct. The night sequence is quietly suspenseful but never scary. The ending is one of the most-loved hugs in children's literature.
