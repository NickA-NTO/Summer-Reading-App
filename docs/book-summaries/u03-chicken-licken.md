# Chicken Licken

**Author:** Traditional folktale, retold for Usborne
**Origin:** English/Scottish folktale (an early printed version, "The Story of Chicken-Licken," appeared in 1849)
**Grade band:** Grade K / Ages 4–6
**Format:** Illustrated retelling for early readers (Usborne)

---

## Premise

A small, panicky chicken called **Chicken Licken** is hit on the head by a falling **acorn**. She is sure that **the sky is falling**. She sets off to go and **tell the king**. On her way, she gathers up a string of other farmyard birds, all with rhyming names: **Henny Penny, Cocky Locky, Ducky Lucky, Goosey Loosey, and Turkey Lurkey**. Just when they think they are nearly at the king's palace, a sly **fox** — **Foxy Loxy** — offers to show them the way. He leads them not to the king but **straight to his own den**.

## Setting

A farmyard at first, then a long road through the countryside on the way to the king's palace. The story ends in the fox's **lair** in the woods. The setting is timeless and storybook.

## Characters

- **Chicken Licken** — a small, nervous hen. The one hit on the head by the acorn.
- **Henny Penny** — a hen friend who joins the journey.
- **Cocky Locky** — a rooster.
- **Ducky Lucky** — a duck.
- **Goosey Loosey** — a goose.
- **Turkey Lurkey** — a turkey.
- **Foxy Loxy** — a sly, smiling fox.
- **The king** — the friends are going to tell him their bad news. He never appears in the story.

## Plot in order

1. **A walk in the wood.** **Chicken Licken** is scratching about under a tree when an **acorn** falls from a branch and bonks her on the head.
2. **"The sky is falling!"** Chicken Licken is sure that the sky is falling. She decides to go and **tell the king** at once.
3. **Meeting Henny Penny.** On the road she meets **Henny Penny**, who asks where she is going. Chicken Licken says: **"The sky is falling and I am going to tell the king!"** Henny Penny says, "I will come with you."
4. **Meeting Cocky Locky.** Next, they meet **Cocky Locky**. He asks where they are going. They tell him. He says, "I will come with you."
5. **Meeting Ducky Lucky.** Then they meet **Ducky Lucky**. Same question, same answer. He joins the line.
6. **Meeting Goosey Loosey.** Then **Goosey Loosey** joins.
7. **Meeting Turkey Lurkey.** Then **Turkey Lurkey** joins.
8. **Meeting Foxy Loxy.** At last they meet a sly red fox — **Foxy Loxy**. He hears their story and tells them: "I know a shortcut to the king's palace. Follow me." The birds are very pleased.
9. **Into the woods.** Foxy Loxy leads the line of birds **off the road and into the woods**.
10. **The fox's den.** The shortcut, of course, is not to the palace. Foxy Loxy is leading them straight to his own **lair** — where his hungry **cubs** are waiting.
11. **Eaten up.** The fox shuts the birds into his den. Chicken Licken, Henny Penny, Cocky Locky, Ducky Lucky, Goosey Loosey and Turkey Lurkey are all **eaten** by the fox and his family.
12. **The king never finds out.** The king never gets the news that the sky is falling — because of course it never was. The sky was never falling at all; just one small acorn.

> *In some softer retellings, the birds spot the trick at the last minute and escape. The classic version above is the older one.*

## Key objects and props

- **The acorn** that bops Chicken Licken on the head.
- **The road to the king's palace**.
- **Foxy Loxy's "shortcut"** through the woods.
- **The fox's lair / den**.

## Themes and ideas

- **Believing rumours without checking.** Chicken Licken is convinced the sky is falling based on one small acorn.
- **A panic spreads.** Every animal she meets believes her without question.
- **Trusting strangers can be dangerous.** Foxy Loxy is sly and helpful-sounding, but he is leading them to disaster.
- **Cumulative storytelling.** The book builds up a longer and longer line of birds, with the same rhyming exchange every time.
- **A funny moral.** Don't panic; check your facts; don't follow strange foxes.

## Tone and style

A short, rhyming, repeating folktale retold for young children. Every new bird that joins is introduced with the same back-and-forth: "Where are you going?" "To tell the king the sky is falling!" "I will come with you." Bright cartoon illustrations show the line of birds growing longer. Foxy Loxy is drawn as a sly red fox with a wide grin.
