# Owl at Home

**Author:** Arnold Lobel
**First published:** 1975
**Grade band:** Grade 1 / Ages 4–8
**Format:** Easy-reader chapter book (five short stories)

---

## Premise

Five gentle, slightly silly short stories about a sweet and slightly anxious **Owl** who lives by himself in a small house. Owl gets into small misadventures — letting Winter inside, being afraid of the bumps at the bottom of his bed, making tea out of tears, trying to be in two places at once, and befriending the moon. Every story stands alone but they all share the same kindly tone.

## Setting

Owl's own house — a cosy home with a fire, a chair, a bed upstairs, a staircase, a kettle and a pea-soup pot. One of the stories also takes place at **the seashore**, on a **large rock**, looking out at the moon.

## Characters

- **Owl** — the only named main character; appears in every story. Lives alone, makes his own meals, talks to himself, and is the gentle, worried hero of every chapter.
- **Winter** — appears as a guest in the first story. Not a person, but treated as a character who can knock on the door, be invited in, and be shouted out.
- **The Moon** — appears in the last story as Owl's friend. Also treated as a character rather than just scenery.

No other named characters appear in the book.

## The five stories, in order

### 1. The Guest

- Owl is at home by his fire when something knocks at the door. He invites his visitor in — it turns out to be **Winter** himself.
- Owl politely sits Winter down by the fire and serves him **buttered toast** and a bowl of **hot pea soup**.
- Winter is a terrible guest. He **blows out the fire**, **whirls snow up the stairs**, blows down the hallway, makes the **window shades flap**, **turns the pea soup into hard green ice**, and covers everything with snow.
- Owl finally shouts at Winter to **leave**, and Winter rushes out and **slams the door**.
- Owl puts the fire back, settles into his chair, and **quietly finishes his supper**.

### 2. Strange Bumps

- In bed at night, Owl notices **two strange bumps** at the **bottom of his bed**, under the blanket.
- He becomes afraid they might **grow bigger in the night**.
- He tries to get rid of them: he **pulls all the covers off**, **moves his feet up and down**, and **jumps on the bed** — which makes **the bed crash down**.
- Frightened, Owl goes **downstairs** and sleeps in his **chair near the fire**.
- The reader (and Owl) eventually realise the two bumps were just **his own two feet**.

### 3. Tear-Water Tea

- Owl decides to make a special drink called **tear-water tea**.
- He puts a **kettle on his lap** and thinks of sad things until he cries enough tears to fill it.
- The sad things he lists include: **chairs with broken legs**, **songs that cannot be sung because the words are forgotten**, **spoons that have fallen behind the stove**, **books with torn pages**, **clocks that have stopped**, **mornings nobody saw because everyone was asleep**, **mashed potatoes left on a plate that nobody wanted to eat**, and **pencils that are too short to use**.
- His tears drip into the kettle until it is full.
- Owl then boils the kettle and drinks his tea. He says it tastes **"a little bit salty"** — but he likes it.

### 4. Upstairs and Downstairs

- Owl has a staircase in his house with **twenty steps**.
- He decides he would like to be **upstairs and downstairs at the same time**.
- He runs up and down the stairs over and over, trying to manage it.
- Tired out, he finally **sits down on the tenth step** — the middle of the staircase — and rests, deciding the middle is the best he can do.

### 5. Owl and the Moon

- Owl is at the **seashore**, sitting on a **large rock**, when he notices the **moon** rising over the water.
- He talks to the moon and tells it **"We must be very good friends."**
- A cloud covers the moon, and Owl feels sad — he thinks the moon has gone away.
- The moon comes back out from behind the cloud.
- Owl says goodbye to the moon, saying it is always **a little sad to say goodbye to a friend**.
- He walks home, and as he climbs into bed the moon shines through his bedroom window. Owl no longer feels sad, and he falls asleep.

## Key objects and props

- **The fire** in Owl's hearth — important in The Guest and Strange Bumps.
- **Buttered toast** — what Owl serves Winter.
- **Hot pea soup** — what Owl serves Winter; Winter freezes it into "hard green ice".
- **Owl's bed** — the bed Owl jumps on and that crashes down in Strange Bumps.
- **The kettle** — Owl cries into it to make tear-water tea.
- **The staircase with twenty steps** — Owl runs up and down it in Upstairs and Downstairs.
- **The tenth step** — where Owl finally sits down.
- **The large rock at the seashore** — where Owl meets the moon.
- **The bedroom window** — where the moon shines in at the very end.

## Themes and ideas

- **Being on your own** — Owl lives by himself; the stories celebrate quiet, solitary domestic life.
- **Small worries and small comforts** — many of Owl's troubles are imaginary or self-made, and the comforts are small (a chair by the fire, a cup of tea, the moon shining in).
- **Feelings** — being scared, being sad, being lonely, being satisfied — each story gently names a feeling.
- **Friendship** — Owl finds a friend in the moon and is sad when it goes away.
- **Trying to do too much** — the Upstairs and Downstairs story is about accepting you can't be in two places at once.

## Tone and style

Short, calm, slightly funny prose with soft watercolour illustrations. Sentences are short and the same shapes repeat, making the book very easy to read for a beginner. Owl talks to himself a lot, which gives the stories their gentle humour. The book is meant to be read at bedtime — the rhythm is slow and reassuring.
