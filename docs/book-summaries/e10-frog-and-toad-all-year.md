# Frog and Toad All Year

**Author and Illustrator:** Arnold Lobel
**First published:** 1976
**Grade band:** Pre-K / Early reader
**Format:** *I Can Read* easy-reader chapter book (third book in the Frog and Toad series)

---

## Premise

Five short stories about the two best friends **Frog** and **Toad**, one for each season of the year. They go sledding in **winter**, look for **spring** around corners, eat melting **ice cream** in summer, secretly rake each other's leaves in **autumn**, and try (and very nearly fail) to celebrate **Christmas Eve** together.

## Setting

A gentle countryside world, with Frog and Toad's two small houses near each other. The stories move through the seasons:

- A **snowy hill** in winter.
- Toad's house and the road in spring.
- A **lake** and an **ice-cream stand** in summer.
- Each other's gardens, full of **fallen leaves**, in autumn.
- Toad's house on **Christmas Eve**, with a path through dark woods to Frog's house.

## Characters

- **Frog** — green, taller, calmer. Often has the idea that starts a story.
- **Toad** — brown, shorter, easily worried. The one who panics and fusses, but loves Frog deeply.

No other named characters appear, although in "Christmas Eve" Toad imagines a **coyote**.

## The five stories, in order

### 1. Down the Hill (Winter)

- **Frog** turns up at Toad's house with a **sled** and tells Toad it is a beautiful day for sledding.
- **Toad** does not want to go sledding at all.
- Frog gets him out into the snow and they go up to the top of a hill.
- They climb onto the sled and start down. Halfway down, **Frog falls off**. Toad keeps going, alone, on a sled he cannot steer.
- Toad has a long, terrified ride to the bottom of the hill, sure he is going to crash. He crashes into a snowbank instead.
- Toad declares he will spend the rest of the winter **safely in bed**.

### 2. The Corner (Spring)

- It is raining at Toad's house. Toad is restless, waiting for **spring** to arrive.
- Frog cheers him up by telling a story from when he was little.
- When Frog was a small frog, his father told him spring was waiting **just around the corner**.
- Little Frog went looking for spring around every corner — round the pine tree, around the house, around the garden — but he could not find it.
- Finally, going home, he came round one more corner and **found spring** at last: the rain stopped, the sun came out, the leaves were green.
- The story ends Toad's gloomy rainy day on a hopeful note.

### 3. Ice Cream (Summer)

- One **hot** summer's day, Toad walks down to the ice-cream stand to buy **two chocolate ice-cream cones** — one for him and one for Frog.
- On the long walk back, the ice cream begins to **melt** all over him.
- Toad keeps trying to hold both cones, but the melted ice cream covers his arms, his face, and his head.
- By the time Toad arrives at the lake where Frog is waiting, Toad is covered in chocolate ice cream from head to foot. He looks so strange that **other animals** see him and **run away in fright**.
- Toad falls into the lake to wash off. He and Frog go back to the stand and buy new cones — and eat them fast this time.

### 4. The Surprise (Autumn)

- It is **autumn** and the leaves have fallen. **Frog** decides to secretly walk over to Toad's house and **rake all of Toad's leaves** as a surprise.
- At the same time, **Toad** secretly walks over to Frog's house and rakes **all of Frog's leaves** as a surprise.
- Each of them finishes the job and goes home, very pleased with themselves and never knowing they passed each other.
- That night the **autumn wind** blows the carefully raked leaves **back across both gardens**.
- In the morning, both Frog and Toad look out at gardens just as full of leaves as before. **Neither of them ever knows** about the other's secret gift — but in their hearts, they both know they are great friends.

### 5. Christmas Eve (Winter)

- It is **Christmas Eve**. Toad is at home, expecting Frog to come for dinner. **Frog is late.**
- Toad starts to worry. He imagines that Frog has **fallen into a deep, dark hole**.
- He worries more. He imagines Frog has **got lost in the woods**.
- He worries even more. He imagines Frog has been **attacked by a coyote** ("a big animal with sharp teeth").
- Toad bravely picks up a **rope** (to pull Frog out of the hole), a **lantern** (to find him in the dark woods), and a **frying pan** (to hit the coyote with).
- Just as Toad rushes outside to rescue Frog, **Frog arrives at the door**, calm and on time after all. He had only been a few minutes late because he was wrapping Toad's present.
- They have a warm Christmas Eve together.

## Key objects and props

- **Frog's sled** — the cause of all the trouble in Story 1.
- **The big hill** they sled down.
- **The corner** — and the spring that lives around it in Story 2.
- **Two melting chocolate ice-cream cones** in Story 3.
- **The lake** — where Toad washes the ice cream off.
- **The autumn wind** — the villain of Story 4.
- **The rope, the lantern, and the frying pan** — Toad's rescue kit in Story 5.

## Themes and ideas

- **Friendship through the seasons.** The book is one whole year of being best friends.
- **Patience.** Toad waits for spring; Frog waits at the bottom of the hill.
- **Kind secret gifts.** "The Surprise" is a beautifully understated story about doing kind things for somebody who never finds out.
- **Imagination and worry.** Toad's mind goes to the worst place every time. Frog never does.
- **Things turn out all right.** Each story has a small panic and a happy ending.

## Tone and style

Calm, slow, gently funny prose. Short sentences and repeating shapes — perfect for a beginning reader. Arnold Lobel's soft watercolours show two friends in jackets and trousers having very domestic adventures.
