# Should I Share My Ice Cream?

**Author and Illustrator:** Mo Willems
**First published:** 2011
**Grade band:** Pre-K / Beginning reader
**Format:** Easy-reader picture book (part of the Elephant & Piggie series)

---

## Premise

**Gerald** the elephant has a delicious **ice-cream cone**. He thinks how wonderful it would taste — and then he realises Piggie isn't there. Should he **share** his ice cream with her? He stops to think about it. And keeps thinking. And keeps thinking. While he is thinking, the **ice cream slowly melts** in the sun. By the time Gerald decides to share, the ice cream is a **puddle on the ground**. But just as he's feeling guilty, **Piggie** shows up — with her own ice-cream cone — and offers to share hers with him.

## Setting

Outdoors on a sunny day, on a blank white background. The whole book happens in a single scene, with Gerald standing on the page holding his cone.

## Characters

- **Gerald** — the elephant. The one with the ice cream. The one who keeps thinking.
- **Piggie** — the pig. Off-page for most of the book; arrives at the end with her own ice cream.

## Plot in order

1. **A wonderful treat.** Gerald has a beautiful tall **ice-cream cone**. He is going to enjoy it.
2. **A second thought.** Gerald notices Piggie is not there. He starts to think about whether he should **share** it with her when she comes back.
3. **Thinking and thinking.** Gerald imagines what Piggie would say. He imagines her being happy he saved her some. He imagines her being upset if he didn't. He goes back and forth.
4. **Drip.** While Gerald thinks, the **ice cream begins to melt** in the sun. A few drops fall off the cone.
5. **More melting.** Gerald keeps debating with himself. The ice cream **keeps melting** — bigger drips, then a stream, then a puddle.
6. **Decision time.** Gerald finally decides: yes, he wants to **share** his ice cream with Piggie.
7. **Too late.** By the time he looks at his cone, **all of the ice cream is gone**. The cone is empty. There is a puddle on the ground. He has "blown it."
8. **Piggie arrives.** Just as Gerald is feeling sad, **Piggie** runs up — with **her own ice-cream cone** in her trotter.
9. **Piggie shares.** Without a moment's thought, Piggie says she will **share her ice cream** with Gerald.
10. **A happy ending.** Gerald is amazed and grateful. He didn't get to enjoy his own ice cream, and he didn't get to share it the way he planned, but his best friend has shared hers with him.

## Key objects and props

- **Gerald's ice-cream cone** — the centre of the book. Slowly melts to nothing across the pages.
- **The puddle of melted ice cream** at Gerald's feet at the end.
- **Piggie's ice-cream cone** — the unexpected gift at the end.

## Themes and ideas

- **Sharing.** The whole book is one long question about whether Gerald should share.
- **Thinking too long.** Gerald spends so much time deciding that he loses what he was deciding about.
- **Generosity comes back to you.** Piggie cheerfully shares with Gerald, exactly as he was hoping to do for her.
- **Friendship without keeping score.** Piggie doesn't know about Gerald's drama; she just shares.
- **The melting ice cream.** A funny, vivid way of showing that decisions have a time limit.

## Tone and style

A very short, very funny picture book. Mostly Gerald talking to himself in big thought-bubbles. The drawing of his **ice cream slowly shrinking** across the pages is the joke. The book is meant to be read in one sitting and re-read often.
