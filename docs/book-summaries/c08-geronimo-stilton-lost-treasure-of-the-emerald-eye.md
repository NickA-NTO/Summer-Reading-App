# Geronimo Stilton: Lost Treasure of the Emerald Eye

**Author:** Elisabetta Dami (writing as Geronimo Stilton)
**First English edition:** 2004 (originally published in Italian)
**Grade band:** Grade 3 / Ages 7–10
**Format:** Illustrated early chapter book (first book in the English Geronimo Stilton series)

---

## Premise

**Geronimo Stilton** is a mouse and the editor of a newspaper in **New Mouse City**. He likes a quiet life — a cup of hot cheese tea, his books, his newspaper. But when his adventurous sister **Thea** turns up with a **mysterious old treasure map**, Geronimo is dragged out of his cosy office on a long sea voyage with Thea, his loud cousin **Trap**, and his sweet little nephew **Benjamin**. They sail to a faraway island in search of buried treasure. After a lot of seasickness, an unexpected stowaway, and many ridiculous misadventures, they discover that the "**Emerald Eye**" of the title is not a jewel at all — it is a beautiful **green, eye-shaped lake** on a tropical island. The "lost" island turns out to be a busy holiday spot full of tourists and a hotel.

## Setting

Two worlds:

- **New Mouse City** on **Mouse Island** — a city of mice where Geronimo lives and works. The story opens in **The Rodent's Gazette** newspaper office.
- **The open sea and a tropical island** — the rest of the book. Geronimo's group sails on a ship, lands on the island, hikes through it, and discovers it is full of tourists.

## Characters

- **Geronimo Stilton** — a mouse. The **editor of The Rodent's Gazette**, the most popular newspaper in New Mouse City. The narrator. He is gentle, anxious, a little snobbish, and the worst possible person for an adventure — which is what makes the book funny.
- **Thea Stilton** — Geronimo's **sister**. A daring journalist, a brilliant pilot and explorer. She is the one who finds the map and forces Geronimo on the trip.
- **Trap Stilton** — Geronimo's **cousin**. Loud, greedy, untidy, and always teasing Geronimo. Eats everything in sight.
- **Benjamin Stilton** — Geronimo's young **nephew**. Sweet, clever, polite, and the only one who calls Geronimo "Uncle Geronimo." Geronimo adores him.
- **The crew of the ship** and other characters they meet on the island.

## Plot in order

1. **A quiet afternoon ruined.** Geronimo is at his desk at **The Rodent's Gazette** when his sister **Thea** bursts in, very excited.
2. **The map.** Thea has found an old, mysterious **treasure map** showing a "lost" island and a spot marked with an **X**.
3. **Off on a treasure hunt.** Thea announces they are going. Geronimo protests, but he is overruled. They book passage on a ship and set off.
4. **Family on board.** Geronimo's cousin **Trap** and his nephew **Benjamin** also come along, much to Geronimo's mixed delight and despair.
5. **Seasick Geronimo.** Geronimo is hopeless at sea. He eats some bad **frozen clams** and gets terribly sick. Trap teases him mercilessly.
6. **A stowaway.** They discover a **stowaway** hiding on board — somebody who has slipped onto the ship to come along uninvited.
7. **Finding the island.** With a lot of arguing and shouting from Thea, they navigate to the island marked on the map.
8. **It's not deserted.** When they arrive, the "lost" island turns out to be **full of tourists** — there is a **hotel**, deck chairs, holidaymakers, the whole thing. So much for buried treasure.
9. **Hiking inland.** Determined to follow the map, the group hikes inland looking for the X.
10. **No gold.** Where the X is marked, there is **no chest of gold**. There is only a beautiful **green lake**.
11. **The view from above.** Then somebody (Geronimo, taking a moment to look from a high point) sees the lake from above. From up there, the lake is shaped exactly **like an eye**, and the bright green water makes it look like a giant **emerald**.
12. **The real treasure.** They realise that the "**Lost Treasure of the Emerald Eye**" is not gold or jewels — it is the **lake itself**, a natural wonder.
13. **Home again.** The Stiltons sail back to New Mouse City. Geronimo, still a bit green from the sea, vows he will never go on another adventure — which is, of course, what he says at the end of every book.

## Key objects and props

- **The Rodent's Gazette** — Geronimo's newspaper office in New Mouse City.
- **The treasure map** Thea brings into his office.
- **The X** on the map.
- **The ship** that takes the family to the island.
- **The frozen clams** that make Geronimo seasick.
- **The hotel and deck chairs** on the "lost" island.
- **The green eye-shaped lake** — the actual "Emerald Eye."

## Themes and ideas

- **Family.** Bossy sister, loud cousin, sweet nephew — these four mice are a family, and the book is gently about putting up with them.
- **What "treasure" really is.** The treasure of the title is not gold but a natural wonder.
- **A reluctant hero.** Geronimo is anxious and indoorsy and would much rather be at his desk. The fun of the book is that adventure keeps happening to him anyway.
- **Looking again at familiar things.** From the ground the lake is just a lake; from above it is a treasure.
- **Modern places aren't lost.** The "deserted" island already has tourists and a hotel. The world isn't as wild as old maps make it sound.

## Tone and style

A short, funny chapter book full of cartoon-style **coloured drawings** of mice, ships, and tropical islands. The book is famous for using **lots of different fonts and word art** mid-page — words "shiver" if Geronimo is cold, "wobble" if he's seasick, get **HUGE** when somebody shouts. Each chapter is short. The narrator is Geronimo himself, often complaining to the reader about his sister, his cousin, and his stomach.
