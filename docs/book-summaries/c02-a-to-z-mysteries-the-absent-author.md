# A to Z Mysteries: The Absent Author

**Author:** Ron Roy
**Illustrator:** John Steven Gurney
**First published:** 1997
**Grade band:** Grade 3 / Ages 7–10
**Format:** Short mystery chapter book (first book in the A to Z Mysteries series)

---

## Premise

Three nine-year-old friends — **Dink**, **Josh**, and **Ruth Rose** — try to solve their first real mystery when Dink's favourite author, the famous **Wallis Wallace**, fails to show up at a much-anticipated book signing at the local bookshop. Following clues from the airport, a taxi driver, and the local hotel, the children become convinced that Wallace has been kidnapped. The truth, it turns out, is far cleverer — Wallis Wallace has been right next to them the whole time, in disguise, secretly watching to see how real children would solve the case for the next mystery she is writing.

## Setting

The small fictional town of **Green Lawn, Connecticut**. The action moves between:

- **The Book Nook** — the local bookshop, where Wallace's book signing was supposed to happen.
- **The Shangri-la Hotel** — where Wallace had a hotel room booked.
- **Squires Airport** — where Wallace was supposed to land.
- **An ice-cream parlour** — where a key clue is uncovered.
- **A taxi office** in town.
- **Dink's, Josh's, and Ruth Rose's neighbourhood.**

## Characters

- **Donald David "Dink" Duncan** — the main detective, **nine years old**. The biggest Wallis Wallace fan in town; he has written letters to the author and even sent her his photo.
- **Josh Pinto** — Dink's best friend. Also nine. Cheerful and useful in a chase. Has a dog called Pal.
- **Ruth Rose Hathaway** — Dink's other best friend and next-door neighbour. Nine. Sharp-eyed; she is the one who spots the final clue and works out the twist.
- **Wallis Wallace** — the famous mystery writer. Lives in a castle in **Maine**. Her real identity is the twist of the book.
- **Mavis Green** — a friendly woman in a scarf who turns up just when the kids are confused, claiming she also got a letter from Wallace. (See "The twist.")
- **Mr. Paskey** — the owner of the **Book Nook** bookshop. He is in on the secret.
- **Mr. Linkletter** — the receptionist at the **Shangri-la Hotel**. Stiff, formal, and (it turns out) also in on the secret.
- **Olivia** — a housekeeper at the hotel, who reports that one of Wallace's rooms looked unused.

## Plot in order

1. **A long wait for the author.** Dink has been writing letters to his favourite mystery writer, **Wallis Wallace**, and has invited her to come to Green Lawn for a book signing.
2. **The big day.** On the day of the event, Dink, Josh, and Ruth Rose go to the **Book Nook**, which is packed with fans waiting for Wallace.
3. **The no-show.** Wallace does not arrive. The owner, **Mr. Paskey**, eventually announces the signing has to be **cancelled**.
4. **The first letter.** Dink shows the others a letter Wallace wrote him, in which she promises that the **only reason she would not turn up is if she were kidnapped**.
5. **A second letter.** A woman with a scarf and a soft voice — **Mavis Green** — steps forward in the bookshop and says she also got a letter from Wallace. In her letter, Wallace told Mavis she felt she **was being followed**.
6. **Detectives at work.** Dink, Josh, and Ruth Rose decide to investigate, sure that Wallace has been kidnapped on the way to Green Lawn.
7. **The airport.** They visit **Squires Airport** and confirm that a passenger named Wallis Wallace did arrive on the right flight.
8. **The taxi.** They find the taxi driver who picked Wallace up at the airport. He says he dropped her off at the **Shangri-la Hotel**.
9. **The hotel.** At the **Shangri-la**, the stiff receptionist **Mr. Linkletter** refuses to tell them anything. He does call up to **Room 303** (Wallace's room) but gets no answer.
10. **Sneaking a look at the register.** While Mr. Linkletter is busy, **Josh** sneaks a peek at the hotel register. He sees that **Wallace checked into Room 303 at 8:05 p.m.**, and that another guest, name **smudged**, checked into **Room 302 at 8:15 p.m.** — ten minutes later, right next door.
11. **The housekeeper.** They speak with **Olivia**, the housekeeper, who tells them that **Room 303 looked as if the bed had not even been slept in**. The kids are now sure something bad happened to Wallace.
12. **Around town.** They follow more clues around Green Lawn — including to an **ice-cream parlour**, where they keep seeing Mavis Green.
13. **Ruth Rose's eye.** Ruth Rose starts to notice things about Mavis:
    - Mavis claims Wallace lives in a "cottage." Dink knows Wallace really lives in **a castle in Maine**.
    - Mavis's **scarf** is patterned with the letter **M** — but if you look at it from the side, the **M**s are really **W**s **upside down**.
    - Mavis's favourite **green ice cream**, and her last name **Green**, match Wallace's known **favourite colour**.
14. **The accusation.** Ruth Rose accuses **Mavis** of really being **Wallis Wallace**.
15. **The truth.** Mavis admits it. She **is** Wallis Wallace, in disguise. She often hides her identity to do research without being noticed by fans.
16. **Why she did it.** Wallace explains that she is writing a **new mystery book about children trying to find a missing author**. She wanted to see for herself how a group of real children would investigate, so she set up the disappearance.
17. **Who was in on it.** It turns out **Mr. Paskey** (the bookshop owner) and **Mr. Linkletter** (the hotel receptionist) were both in on the plan, and the "guest" in **Room 302** was actually Wallace's brother.
18. **A clever ending.** Dink, Josh, and Ruth Rose have, without knowing it, helped Wallis Wallace research her next book — and they have solved their first real case.

## Key objects and props

- **Dink's letter from Wallis Wallace** — the original promise that she would only miss the event if kidnapped.
- **Mavis's letter** from Wallace — saying she was being followed.
- **The hotel register** at the Shangri-la — where Josh spots the suspicious neighbouring room.
- **Mavis's scarf** with M's that turn into W's upside down.
- **The green ice cream** at the parlour.
- **Wallace's photo** — which Dink had sent her and now thinks proves she's been "kidnapped."

## Themes and ideas

- **Looking carefully at clues.** Every detail (a letter, a scarf, an order of ice cream) ends up mattering.
- **Working as a team.** Dink, Josh, and Ruth Rose each notice different things. Ruth Rose puts it all together.
- **Things are not always what they seem.** The whole book teaches that disguises and misdirection are part of mysteries.
- **Writers and their stories.** A nice meta-joke — the missing author is, in the end, the author of the mystery.
- **Bravery for nine-year-olds.** The detectives go to airports, taxi offices, and hotels by themselves to solve the case.

## Tone and style

A short, fast, friendly mystery for new chapter-book readers. Each chapter is short, with a small step in the case and a small cliffhanger. Vocabulary is straightforward. Lots of conversation between the three friends. The book is the **A** in an A-to-Z series, so the early chapters are written in a way that introduces the three detectives and the setting in case readers come back for more.
